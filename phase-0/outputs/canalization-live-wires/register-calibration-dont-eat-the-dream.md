# Register Calibration — "Don't Eat the Dream"

**Purpose:** find the *tone* empirically before committing full pages. We've hit two walls — bleak/ironic (sneer) and warm/earnest (sermon). The target is the third thing: **edge + humor sourced from delight**, no posture toward the reader, the joke = the *fit of the analogy*.

**Method:** same beat, three registers, varied only by treatment. Render all three (codex → GPT Image 2), then pick the one you actually *grin* at. Do not run from the riff-mcp side.

**The beat (held constant across all three):** outcome-fantasy is *eating the dream* — you vividly feel it already-yours, get too full/satisfied to act, and the drive (which runs on the maintained absence) dies. The control image is "ate it → too full to move." Only the register changes.

**How to judge:** the grin should come from the *click of the analogy*, not from gloom (Wall 1) or uplift (Wall 2). If it makes you smirk-and-wince at how true it is, the register has no posture — that's the one.

horizon, still hungry.

Two small deadpan caption boxes: "HE ORDERED THE DREAM." / "HE SHOULD NOT HAVE EATEN IT." A
tiny menu footnote by the booth: "recommended serving of your own future: none."

Wide landscape 3:2, high resolution; dry and clean; all lettering legible and correctly spelled.

## After the test

Whichever register wins becomes the house voice for the warm-key line — then we rebuild "The Landscape" and spec the next pages in *that* voice instead of guessing. If none fully grin, the winner + what's missing tells us where the real register sits (e.g. "B's warmth but A's restraint").
