## Reference Authority

Use attached character sheets for likeness and costume:
- Mike sheet: Mike's face, hair, jacket, line economy, simplified rendering.
- Charlie sheet: Charlie's face, hair, beard, white shirt, short/wiry build.
- charlie-ref.png: face and expression reference for Panel 2 only — use for expression quality and tone and framing, not for clothing or setting.
- Prior finished page: page finish, gutters, caption box color and style, lettering, color grouping, print grain, overall comic integration.

Do not invent a new style. Match the series look.

## Series Style

Warm muted printed-comic page.
Clean confident contour lines.
Grouped black shadows.
Simplified forms, designed shapes, restrained interior detail.
Design readable silhouettes and clear foreground / midground / background staging.
No painterly rendering, no photorealism, no glossy modern finish.

All captions:
same handwritten all-caps lettering, same size, same light yellow caption-box tone.
Speech bubbles: same all-caps lettering, clean comic balloon.
Emphasis is heavier ink only, never larger text size.

## Global Continuity

Topanga Canyon commune exterior, 1967. Night throughout, except Panel 1 which has the last thin light at the horizon.

Mike: barely 20, clean-shaven, dark shaggy short hair, lambskin suede jacket, cream t-shirt, straight jeans, dusty brown boots.
Charlie: short, wiry, intense, magnetic — Charles Manson likeness. Shoulder-length wavy dark hair, trimmed full dark beard, white open-collar shirt, dark brown pants. 

The bus: solid black painted conventional school bus — long hood, engine in front of cab. Headlights on throughout. Small hanging Christmas lights along its body. No other markings or decorations.
The bus headlights are the primary light source for Panels 1, 3, and 4. Render the headlight beams as stylized graphic cone shapes — hard-edged flat fills, sharp boundaries, vintage comic style. Not atmospheric glow, not volumetric fog, not gradient falloff. The spotlight circle they motivate on the ground is a designed flat shape, not a naturalistic lighting effect.
Distant commune campfire and small family group remain in the background throughout — secondary, never the focus.

## Panel 2 — Middle Left

Night.
Use charlie-ref.png as the primary reference for framing, angle, and expression in this panel.
Use the Charlie turnaround sheet for costume and build continuity only.
Charlie  (frame left) is playing his trickster archetype — the knowing, slightly amused quality in charlie-ref.png. Running a game, not heavy threat.
BG: deep black night. Charlie has a warm rim of headlight edge glow.
Charlie looks directly at the reader — not toward Mike, not panel-right. Direct camera address.
A hatched halo effect behind Charlie — warm yellow ground rendered through sparse crosshatching near him, increasing in density toward the panel edges where it becomes solid black vignette. Not a glowing aura — a graphic printed effect. black Crosshatching consuming the yellow field: heavy, gestural marks eating into the shape, raw rather than precise, in the manner of Robert Crumb.
