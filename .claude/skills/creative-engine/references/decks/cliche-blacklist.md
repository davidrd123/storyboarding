# Candy-Ad Cliché Blacklist

Source: research/04 (canonical users cited there). These are legal **raw
materials**, banned as **final answers**. During divergence, generate freely;
at Gate 0, tag any seed matching an entry. A tagged seed is not killed — it
must pass through one repair operator before it can advance.

## The tropes

1. **Rainbow / color-explosion / slow-mo flavor burst** — macro juice splash, streaming color (every fruit candy since Skittles)
2. **Bite transports you to a flavor world** — tropical waterfall, candy landscape (the category's most-used mechanic)
3. **Anthropomorphic candy afraid of being eaten** (M&M's lineage)
4. **You become someone else when you eat / until you eat** (Snickers; now category-generic)
5. **Sour-face reaction shot** (every sour candy ever)
6. **Adults act like kids / kid voices** (Haribo owns it; radioactive for anyone else)
7. **The sharing dilemma / possessive hoarding** (Twix, "get your own")
8. **Candy falls from the sky / grows on trees / rains indoors** (Skittles did it first, with tragedy; imitations keep only the rain)
9. **Midas touch — everything touched becomes the candy** (Skittles "Touch")
10. **ASMR unwrap/peel macro with breathy sound design** — ⚠️ for a peelable product this is cliché #1 to fear
11. **Craving possession** — zombie-walk to the vending machine; can't think until eaten
12. **"Random" weirdness with no cost and no rule** — weirdness as wallpaper; the documented AI-slop failure mode
13. **Mascot does extreme sports / is inexplicably cool** (90s residue)
14. **Forbidden/sensual indulgence** — slow-motion ecstasy eating (dated; wrong for a teen product)

## Repair operators (apply exactly one)

- **INVERT** — reverse the trope's direction (the flavor world visits YOU and is disappointed; the tourist climbs OUT of the gummy and photographs the cafeteria)
- **ADD COST** — keep the trope, add the Skittles tragedy beat: someone quietly pays a price for the magic, everyone underreacts
- **RE-CHANNEL** — keep the trope's content but move it to a different channel (voice, physics, scale, texture, lighting, social norms, time — swap exactly one, keep the rest documentary-real)

## The governing warning

"There is no surrealism for surrealism's sake" — every durable surreal spot
has a governing rule + a cost + an underreaction. The engine's novelty comes
from *which* rule and *which* collision; the discipline stays constant.
