# TRIZ 40 inventive principles (Altshuller), glossed for creative work.
# Protocol: state the craft contradiction ("improving X worsens Y"), draw 3
# principles, resolve with BOTH improving — no trade-off allowed.
1. Segmentation — divide it into independent parts; make it modular
2. Taking out — extract the disturbing part or the only necessary part
3. Local quality — make each part serve a different function; no uniformity
4. Asymmetry — replace symmetry with asymmetry; increase existing asymmetry
5. Merging — bring identical or related operations together in space or time
6. Universality — one part performs multiple functions; eliminate the rest
7. Nested doll — place one inside another, inside another
8. Anti-weight — compensate weight by joining with something that lifts
9. Preliminary anti-action — pre-stress against the known future problem
10. Preliminary action — do the required change before it is needed
11. Beforehand cushioning — prepare emergency means in advance
12. Equipotentiality — remove the need to raise or lower; keep it level
13. The other way round — invert the action; fix what moves, move what's fixed
14. Spheroidality — replace straight lines with curves, rollers, rotation
15. Dynamics — make it adjustable, movable, adaptive mid-process
16. Partial or excessive action — do slightly less or absurdly more than needed
17. Another dimension — move into 2D/3D; tilt it; use the reverse side
18. Vibration — oscillate it; use resonance
19. Periodic action — replace continuous action with pulses and pauses
20. Continuity of useful action — eliminate idle strokes; everything works always
21. Skipping — run the harmful or risky step at very high speed
22. Blessing in disguise — use the harmful factor to achieve the positive effect
23. Feedback — introduce feedback; or change the feedback that exists
24. Intermediary — use a carrier or temporary intermediate object
25. Self-service — the thing services itself; waste products do the work
26. Copying — use a cheap copy instead of the fragile expensive original
27. Cheap short-living objects — replace one durable thing with many disposables
28. Mechanics substitution — replace the mechanism with a field, smell, sound
29. Pneumatics and hydraulics — use air, water, inflation instead of solids
30. Flexible shells and thin films — membranes instead of walls
31. Porous materials — make it porous; hide function in the pores
32. Color changes — change color, transparency, visibility
33. Homogeneity — make interacting objects of the same material
34. Discarding and recovering — parts that vanish or restore themselves mid-use
35. Parameter changes — change the state: gas, liquid, solid, concentration
36. Phase transitions — use the moment of transformation itself
37. Thermal expansion — use expansion and contraction
38. Strong oxidants — enrich the atmosphere; intensify the environment
39. Inert atmosphere — calm the environment to nothing; add void
40. Composite materials — combine materials with different properties
