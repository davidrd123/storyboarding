# 0. Ben's open canvas — bigger transformations

This first section deliberately stops treating the current car/parachute and
mango-Voltron answers as requirements. Ben's actual fixed points are where we
meet the talent and the Peelerz Islands finale; everything after product contact
is open. These NB2 Lite probes ask what a “creative situation” looks like when
the transformation reaches architecture, time, filmmaking, scale, mythology,
or cosmology.

![Liz and John remain on campus steps while whole university buildings walk across the grounds](../ideation/visual-probes/batch-20-ben-open-canvas/outputs/2026-07-15/gemini-31-flash-lite-image/01_01-campus-buildings-migrate_d5e83d7a/01-campus-buildings-migrate_01.png)

## Open-canvas questions for Ben

1. How far beyond the current treatment answers should we keep developing?
2. Which scale is most exciting: architectural, temporal, meta-cinematic,
   nested/product-scale, mythic, or cosmological?
3. Should each encounter retain a distinct current destination, or can one of
   these replace a destination entirely?
4. Does the animal-borne football archipelago provide a more surprising route
   into the Peelerz Islands finale?
5. Is the camera-building-the-world idea the right kind of self-aware creative,
   or should the authored-world transformations remain fully inside the story?
6. Do the cleaner reversals—road flies, islands arrive, gummy contains the car—
   feel more useful than the denser cosmological ideas?
7. Is collective behavior a productive Raul direction: becoming the crowd wave,
   being built by headcount, or letting individual gummies make comic choices?
8. Should the Peelerz Islands merely wait at the end, or actively pull, curl,
   rearrange, and welcome the separate story worlds?

# I. Tina

The promising throughline is: Tina examines the lychee gummy in the back seat; the ordinary world becomes a miniature tropical model; a crew of lychee gummies creates seams and unfurls the car; the transformed car material becomes a lychee-pink parasail; Tina is gently ejected or lifted upward; and the parasail can transition into the final rainbow version.

## Tina questions to resolve with Ben

1. Is the primary opener a coordinated lychee crew, one giant hand, the car acting on its own, or a short combination?
2. What precise seam pattern lets the car unfurl cleanly—one roof strip front-to-back, orange-peel segments, lotus petals, or another geometry?
3. How do we show that the car’s substance transforms before it opens, so it never reads as metal being torn away?
4. Do the houses become miniatures first and blossom second, or are those competing world directions?
5. Does the car’s opened material directly become the lychee-pink parasail?
6. What causes Tina’s upward launch, and how gentle/comic should the ejection feel?
7. When does the lychee parasail transition to the rainbow version?
8. Is levitation a shared transition motif across Tina and Raul’s scenes?
9. Is the doppelgänger route worth developing as a separate offbeat option?

# II. Raul + Meg

The main things to solve are:

- What is the transformation mechanism and progression?
- How much open space do we want around Raul as it happens?
- Which angles best sell the transformation, the football-world effect, and Meg’s reaction?
- How do we combine Raul’s power fantasy with the gummies’ cuteness?
- How actively is Meg engaged with Raul and the transformation?

Current overrides for the next pass:

- The football game is in daylight, not dusk.
- The final mecha is made from denser, more opaque mango flesh—not translucent gummy or orange superhero plastic.
- The mango helpers may also be less translucent, keyed to the real mango Peelerz material.
- Existing dusk/translucent images below remain useful for composition, mechanism, and character behavior rather than final lighting or material.

# III. Focused experimental probes

These are discussion images from a constrained creativity-engine pilot, not
proposed final frames. They are included because each offers a more specific
answer—or a more useful failure—to one of the unresolved questions above.

![Four broad pink car-skin sheets rise along lit seams while lychee helpers work around Tina](../ideation/visual-probes/batch-19-render-compiler-test/outputs/2026-07-14/gemini-31-flash-lite-image/01_01-four-attached-car-sheets-follow-moving-seams_7bd098e2/01-four-attached-car-sheets-follow-moving-seams_02.png)

This is the closest probe so far to the car material itself changing before it

![A tactile pink-to-rainbow canopy arc continues into raised field lines around Raul and Meg](../ideation/visual-probes/batch-18-focused-engine-good-weird/outputs/2026-07-14/gemini-31-flash-lite-image/06_06-canopy-arc-becomes-scoreboard-arc_7897d4d0/06-canopy-arc-becomes-scoreboard-arc_01.png)

## Questions raised by the experimental pass

1. Does the scoreboard-replay transformation feel product-specific and fun,
   or does it pull too much attention from the pit-crew build?
2. Should Meg have one sustained physical task across multiple assembly shots,
   such as keeping the next component connector under tension?
3. Is one dedicated helper-scale mechanical insert enough to establish the
   gummies as truly building the mecha?
4. Is the car better understood as sheets following structural ribs than as a
   literal flower/lotus?
5. Does the canopy/scoreboard match cut belong in the film's shared visual
   grammar, even if the current generated image is only a rough ingredient?
