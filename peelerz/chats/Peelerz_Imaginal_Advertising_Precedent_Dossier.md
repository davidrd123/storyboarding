# **01  /  THE BRIEF, CONVERTED INTO A RESEARCH PROBLEM**

The supplied treatment asks for a “wide open canvas” after the candy is eaten—scale, transitions, world-building, flying and “weird” behavior—but imposes a decisive constraint: every event must be sellable in a few visual styleframes. The current structure moves from Tina’s car peeling open and lifting her into flight, to Raul’s mango-anime body transformation, to Liz and John crossing multiple worlds, and finally to a shared Peelerz universe above the clouds. (Treatment, pp. 1–9.)

| THE HISTORICAL PATTERN The most durable “imaginal” commercials are rarely built from unlimited weirdness. They establish one impossible law, then discover its emotional, comic, social or spectacular consequences. |
| :---- |

## **The six findings to carry into ideation**

| 1 | One law beats many effects. Skittles, Sony Bravia and Honda Cog become richer by staying narrower. Each new beat is a consequence, not a new premise. |
| :---: | :---- |
| **2** | **Product behavior is stronger than product presence.** Capri Sun is especially relevant: how the package is handled determines what the consumer becomes. Peelerz has an unusually useful dual behavior—peel or whole. |
| **3** | **Fantasy needs a human desire.** Flight reads as release when the character begins enclosed; transformation reads as self-expression when a social self is established first. |
| **4** | **Multi-world work needs an invariant.** A bottle, camera orbit, handoff, chase, movement or material substrate must survive every change of medium. |
| **5** | **A brand world is a system.** Happiness Factory works because every creature and machine performs a role in the Coke ritual. Giant branded objects alone do not constitute mythology. |
| **6** | **The hero frame is the concept test.** If the operation is not legible at thumbnail size, it will be difficult to pitch, storyboard or remember. |

**Screening question: If another snack can replace Peelerz without changing the visual law, the idea is not finished.**

# **02  /  HOW TO READ THE DOSSIER**

This is a practical precedent map, not a “greatest ads” ranking. The cases were chosen because they preserve a useful written record and because their mechanisms can be translated into the four jobs already present in the Peelerz treatment.

## **Mechanism tags**

| TAG | CONTRACT | WHAT IT MEANS |
| ----- | :---- | :---- |
| **CM** | **Causal magic** | A product action physically causes the impossible event. |
| **MM** | **Metaphoric magic** | A brand or product quality becomes a literal image. |
| **AM** | **Affective magic** | The film behaves the way the product is meant to feel. |
| **WM** | **World-building magic** | The product opens, contains or organizes an alternate system. |
| **BT** | **Body transformation** | The eater’s identity, anatomy or avatar changes. |
| **ET** | **Environment transformation** | A familiar space becomes editable. |
| **MS** | **Medium shift** | The same subject crosses live action, animation, illustration or other visual grammars. |
| **SR** | **Scale rupture** | Scale is the main source of awe or comedy. |
| **CT** | **Collective convergence** | Separate characters or actions combine into one large social image. |

## **Record grades**

**A**  A detailed brand/agency case, creator oral history, production account or strategic document.

**B**  A reliable award archive, brand history or substantial project record.

**C**  A useful contemporary trade article or later retrospective; narrower, but still worth reading.

## **The white-space hypothesis**

| PEEL | Progressive · layered · revealing · assembling · unspooling · deliberate |
| :---: | :---- |
| **WHOLE** | **Immediate · total · explosive · scale-changing · gravity-breaking · impulsive** |

*No case in the 26 exactly owns this dual-action system. Honda’s The Other Side is the closest structural precedent because the viewer’s action selects between two synchronized realities. That gap is potentially Peelerz’s most ownable territory.*

| 01  GUSHERS  /  Fruit Heads   1990s |  |
| :---- | :---- |
| **MECHANISM**  CM · BT · SR | **PEELERZ MATCH**  RAUL |
| **WRITTEN RECORD  /  GRADE B**  [General Mills brand history](https://www.generalmills.com/news/stories/the-history-of-general-mills-fruit-snacks) |  |
| **CENTRAL VISUAL LAW**  Eating the fruit snack instantly changes the eater’s head into the matching fruit. | **HERO FRAME**  A normal kid’s body topped by an enormous, unmistakable fruit head. |

| **RISK TO AVOID**  Literal fruitification is already a well-known category trope. Borrow the causal clarity, not the fruit-head surface. |  |

## **Vignette opportunity map**

| TINA  /  ESCAPE | Whole-eat can produce an instantaneous total release: the car’s outer shell peels off in one continuous skin and becomes the device that carries her. Keep the car as the visual anchor while its geometry edits progressively. Emotional truth: release from enclosure. |
| :---- | :---- |
| **RAUL  /  AVATAR** | Peeling can produce staged construction: each strip becomes a plate, limb, aura or graphic layer. His transformation should express a social desire—visibility, confidence, self-authorship—not merely parody anime mechanics. |
| **LIZ \+ JOHN  /  CONNECTION** | Use the circular dolly as a seam. Each orbit removes one layer of campus or fuses one layer from each person. Keep one invariant across every medium: hands, eye line, candy, peel ribbon or matched gesture. Emotional truth: two private realities discovering compatibility. |
| **FINALE  /  CONVERGENCE** | Do not simply gather everyone in Candyland. Let their distinct grammars interlock: Tina’s ribbon, Raul’s armor layers and the couple’s merged world-sheet combine to peel open the clouds and reveal a functioning Peelerz universe. |

## **Recommended campaign law**

| EVERY ORDINARY THING HAS ANOTHER WORLD UNDER ITS SURFACE. Peeling reveals and constructs that hidden world progressively. Eating whole breaks through to it all at once. |

# **09  /  WORKING CHECKLIST**

|  | TEST | QUESTION |
| ----- | :---- | :---- |
| **□** | **ONE-SECOND READ** | Can the hero frame be understood before anyone explains it? |
| **□** | **VISIBLE TRIGGER** | Can we see the peel, bite, whole-eat or shared handling that causes the event? |
| **□** | **ONE VERB** | Can the transformation be named with one dominant verb: peels, unspools, assembles, reveals, snaps, inflates, flips? |
| **□** | **PRODUCT PHYSICS** | Does the effect inherit Peelerz’s layers, stretch, shine, tear, center or two eating modes? |
| **□** | **HUMAN FANTASY** | What desire does the image resolve: escape, visibility, connection or belonging? |
| **□** | **INVARIANT** | Across changing worlds, what remains stable enough to orient the viewer? |
| **□** | **ESCALATION** | Does each beat increase scale or consequence without introducing a new premise? |
| **□** | **TRANSITION VALUE** | Does the image also create the exit into the next scene? |
| **□** | **APPETITE** | Does the candy still look edible, tactile and desirable? |
| **□** | **REPLACEABILITY** | Would the same idea work unchanged for another snack? If yes, push the product logic further. |

**A styleframe-ready sentence template**

| ORDINARY SETUP  \+  PRODUCT ACTION  \+  ONE IMPOSSIBLE OPERATION  \+  EMOTIONAL PAYOFF  \+  TRANSITION OUT Example: Raul removes one mango peel strip; it whips around his arm and locks into translucent armor; each new strip builds a larger avatar until the final plate becomes the circular seam that spins us into Liz and John’s campus. |
