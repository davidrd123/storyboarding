## The unifying campaign rule

The most useful creative rule is:

> **Everything ordinary has another world underneath it. Peelerz lets you peel or break through to the center.**

That rule can govern every sequence without forcing every image to look identical:

The car has another form underneath its shell.

Raul has a heightened avatar underneath his everyday appearance.

The campus contains successive visual worlds beneath its surface.

The sky contains the shared Peelerz universe beneath the clouds.

This is much stronger than merely repeating fruit shapes or using the same color palette. It provides a common **logic** while allowing photorealism, anime, graphic animation, underwater imagery, and cosmic scale to coexist.

## How to judge any proposed styleframe

A viable idea should pass this compact test:

| Question | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Can the transformation be understood in one second? | The treatment must sell through a few stills. |
| Is Peelerz visibly responsible? | Otherwise it is generic fantasy advertising. |
| Does the transformation follow one rule? | Multiple unrelated gimmicks create AI-demo clutter. |
| Does it express escape, identity, connection, or belonging? | Weirdness needs an emotional reason. |
| Is it age-appropriate for the late-teen/college-coded world? | Too cute or childish will undermine the casting. |
| Does it occupy the correct scale in the escalation? | Early scenes should not eclipse the finale. |
| Does it contain an exit or transition? | These are moving sequences, not standalone posters. |
| Could another snack replace Peelerz without changing the idea? | If yes, the concept needs more product physics. |

## The actual ideation assignment

The job is not to create a huge collection of disconnected surreal images. It is to generate **alternative visual mechanisms** for four defined functions:

**How Tina’s contained world peels open and launches her.**

**How Raul’s peeling action constructs a larger-than-life identity.**

**How Liz and John’s connection progressively changes or removes layers of their world.**

**How all three visual grammars converge into one final Peelerz universe.**

For each proposal, the useful deliverable is:

> **One-sentence setup → product trigger → transformation rule → hero frame → transition out**

That keeps every idea concise enough for the requested styleframes while preventing the work from becoming random spectacle.

## Bottom line

The real container is **tightly structured maximalism**.

It is not “anything can happen.” It is:

> **A youth-coded world in which Peelerz transforms an object, then a person, then an entire shared reality, before revealing that all of those transformations belong to one peelable universe.**

The strongest contribution will be making every transformation **simpler, more product-specific, more emotionally motivated, and less dependent on generic candyland or borrowed genre imagery**.

## 2. The closest public records of what other creatives actually made

The strongest practical documents are industry case studies, award records, scripts, production retrospectives and oral histories. They reveal the brief, strategic leap, execution and client resistance more clearly than most academic writing.

### **Gushers — Fruit Heads**

The official General Mills history recalls the 1990s campaign in which children ate Gushers and their heads transformed into pieces of fruit. [![](https://www.google.com/s2/favicons?domain=https://www.generalmills.se&sz=128)General Mills Sweden](https://www.generalmills.se/news/stories/the-history-of-general-mills-fruit-snacks?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

**Imaginal mechanism:** consumption causes bodily transformation.

**Why it matters here:** this is probably the most direct category ancestor of Raul. It demonstrates how a snack’s fruit content can become a literal mutation of the eater. It is also a warning: fruit-body transformation has already been culturally claimed quite memorably, so “person becomes mango” needs a specifically Peelerz mechanic—layers, assembly, peeling, or an outer/inner identity—rather than fruit substitution alone.

### **Capri Sun — Disrespectoids / Respect the Pouch**

Communication Arts describes a multi-year campaign in which children who mishandled the pouch were transformed into physical manifestations of the exact way they had “disrespected” it. The stated objective was to generate youth and teen desire around the pouch itself. [![](https://www.google.com/s2/favicons?domain=https://www.commarts.com&sz=128)Communication Arts](https://www.commarts.com/project/22773/capri-sun-disrespectoids?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

**Imaginal mechanism:** product ritual creates a personalized avatar or punishment.

**Why it matters here:** the transformation is not interchangeable. The distinctive packaging behavior writes the character design. That is a particularly strong model for Peelerz: how someone peels, bites, stretches, separates or eats the candy could determine what they become.

### **Skittles — “Touch”**

The full script is preserved by Communication Arts. Tim has a Midas-like condition in which anything he touches turns into Skittles; a stapler, telephone and eventually his desk all obey the same rule. The joke develops because the apparently wonderful power has terrible implications for ordinary life. [![](https://www.google.com/s2/favicons?domain=https://www.commarts.com&sz=128)Communication Arts](https://www.commarts.com/project/14810/skittles-touch-tv-commercial?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

**Imaginal mechanism:** one brand property becomes a universal physical law.

**Why it matters here:** it is an unusually pure example of **one impossible rule, repeatedly consequentialized**. The creators do not add portals, avatars, flying and animation changes. They ask, “What would actually happen if everything you touched became Skittles?” The resulting world feels richer because it is narrower.

### **Coca-Cola — “Happiness Factory”**

The One Club record describes a vending-machine purchase becoming a journey through an animated factory world in which fantastical creatures transport, cap, chill and celebrate the bottle before it enters reality. [![](https://www.google.com/s2/favicons?domain=https://www.oneclub.org&sz=128)The One Club](https://www.oneclub.org/awards/theoneshow/-award/5667/happiness-factory/)

**Imaginal mechanism:** an invisible mundane process conceals a complete brand civilization.

**Why it matters here:** this is the clearest precedent for the Peelerz cloud-world finale. The alternate world succeeds because every creature, machine and ritual has one purpose: producing the Coke. It is not a general fantasy land that happens to contain the product.

For Peelerz, that raises a productive question:

> Is the final sky world merely made of candy, or is it a world that could only have been generated by peeling?

### **Sony Bravia — “Balls”**

D&AD’s unusually detailed case study follows the work from product strategy through script, logistics, filming and impact. The strategic claim became “Colour Like No Other”; the winning execution was essentially reducible to releasing an immense quantity of colored balls down San Francisco’s hills. No computer-generated balls were needed. [![](https://www.google.com/s2/favicons?domain=https://www.dandad.org&sz=128)D&AD](https://www.dandad.org/insights/awards/sony-balls-case-study-insights)

**Imaginal mechanism:** one product benefit becomes an enormous physical spectacle.

**Why it matters here:** it is the best discipline for the styleframe assignment. Its entire concept can be understood through one sentence and one image. Large-scale imagination does not require conceptual complexity.

The relevant lesson is:

> **Scale should amplify a simple thought, not compensate for the absence of one.**

### **Guinness — “Surfer”**

The wave’s energy is embodied by enormous white horses moving through the water alongside the surfers. Framestore’s account describes the horses as the physical spirit of the wave. [![](https://www.google.com/s2/favicons?domain=https://www.framestore.com&sz=128)Framestore](https://www.framestore.com/work/guinness?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

**Imaginal mechanism:** metaphor becomes materially real.

**Why it matters here:** the white horses are not random surreal content. They connect the white crest, the violence and patience of the sea, and Guinness’s established communication around waiting. A later retrospective argues that the commercial’s unnecessary mythic excess is precisely where craft elevates a basic proposition into something culturally durable. [![](https://www.google.com/s2/favicons?domain=https://creative.salon&sz=128)Creative Salon](https://creative.salon/articles/features/guinness-surfer-learnings-qotw?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

For Peelerz, a comparable move might not be “place mango objects everywhere.” It might be identifying the **spirit of peeling**—release, revelation, shedding, unspooling, discovering another self—and giving that spirit physical form.

### **Cadbury Dairy Milk — “Gorilla”**

D&AD’s case study contains the original strategic progression, client concern, research response, production history and results. Fallon’s premise was that the communication should create the same pleasurable feeling as eating the chocolate. There is no chocolate demonstration and no causal explanation for why a gorilla is playing drums. The larger platform was broad enough to contain anything comparably enjoyable. [![](https://www.google.com/s2/favicons?domain=https://www.dandad.org&sz=128)D&AD](https://www.dandad.org/insights/awards/cadbury-gorilla-case-study-insights)

**Imaginal mechanism:** the film does not illustrate the product; it reproduces the product’s emotional effect.

**Why it matters here:** this is the counterargument to making every Peelerz transformation mechanically literal. A commercial can be product-specific through **emotional equivalence** rather than physical causality.

But Cadbury had a highly disciplined emotional criterion: does this feel as good as eating the product? “Anything enjoyable” was not really an open canvas; it was a strict affective filter.

### **PlayStation 2 — “The Third Place”**

The David Lynch commercial and its surviving making-of materials position PlayStation as entry into an additional, strange experiential realm beyond ordinary life. Sony’s marketing director said Lynch was appropriate because he seemed already to inhabit that kind of place. [![](https://www.google.com/s2/favicons?domain=https://welcometotwinpeaks.com&sz=128)Welcome to Twin Peaks+1](https://welcometotwinpeaks.com/lynch/david-lynch-playstation-commercial-making-of/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

**Imaginal mechanism:** the brand is a portal into dream logic rather than a product that performs one discrete transformation.

**Why it matters here:** it is useful for the Liz and John sequence, where the pleasure may be less about a single transformation and more about moving through incompatible ontologies—live action, animation, underwater space and weightlessness. Its main lesson is that dream logic still needs a stable emotional atmosphere. Lynch’s world is fragmented, but not aesthetically arbitrary.

## 3. Four recurring “imaginal contracts”

The precedents are easier to use when separated by what they ask the audience to believe.

### **1. Causal magic**

> The product action physically causes the impossible event.

Gushers, Capri Sun and Skittles operate this way. The benefit is ownership: remove the product and the scene collapses.

For Peelerz, this is strongest for Tina and Raul. A peel can become armor, a ribbon, a parachute, a removed layer of architecture or an opening in reality.

### **2. Metaphoric magic**

> An intangible product or brand quality becomes a literal image.

Sony turns “better color” into a flood of color. Guinness turns power, patience and white foam into horses.

For Peelerz, this could mean translating **choice**, **revelation**, **play**, **two textures**, or **an inside and outside** into physical laws.

### **3. Affective magic**

> The commercial behaves the way consuming the product feels.

Cadbury Gorilla is the clearest example. Product causality is unnecessary because the entertainment itself is the demonstration.

This would support genuinely freeform Peelerz ideas, but they would still require a precise emotional criterion—perhaps satisfying release, mischievous possibility or tactile pleasure.

### **4. World-building magic**

> The product contains, opens or grants entry into a coherent alternate system.

Happiness Factory and The Third Place operate here.

The Liz and John section and the final Peelerz cloud universe clearly belong to this tradition. Their challenge is coherence: what rules, materials, creatures and transitions make this a **Peelerz world**, rather than a general collection of attractive AI universes?

## 4. The historical lesson for this treatment

The treatment currently moves among all four contracts:

**Tina:** causal and metaphoric magic—consumption opens the enclosed vehicle and grants release.

**Raul:** causal magic—consumption generates an alternate body.

**Liz and John:** affective and world-building magic—connection transports them through changing realities.

**Finale:** brand mythology—the separate private fantasies are revealed as parts of one shared universe. Copy of PEELERZ - IDEATION POSS…

That range is not inherently a problem. The historical precedents suggest, however, that the memorable executions normally possess:

> **One impossible law at the center, followed by several inventive consequences.**

They are less often built from several unrelated impossible laws:

Sony: color pours through the real world.

Skittles: touch converts matter into candy.

Guinness: the wave contains horses.

Coke: the vending machine contains a hidden civilization.

Gushers: eating fruit candy fruitifies the eater.

Capri Sun: interaction with the package determines bodily transformation.

Cadbury: the entertainment must reproduce the pleasure of the product.

That may be the single most useful principle for evaluating Peelerz ideas. “Could this be summed up in a few styleframes?” is partly a production constraint, but it is also a conceptual test. A styleframe can capture a **law** very effectively; it struggles to explain an accumulation of unrelated incidents.

## The most useful conclusion

There is a real canon here, and it gives us a better term than “weird creative situation.”

The tradition is about:

> **Inventing an impossible rule that converts a product truth or product feeling into a visible world.**

For Peelerz, the most directly applicable lineage is **Gushers + Capri Sun + Skittles**: product interaction produces a specific transformation. The best discipline comes from **Sony + Guinness**: a huge image is built around one legible thought. **Coke** supplies the model for a coherent brand universe, while **Cadbury** establishes the alternative case in which the situation need only reproduce the emotional pleasure of eating.

The next useful research artifact would be a **20–30-commercial precedent dossier**, tagged by imaginal mechanism, with the surviving written record, central visual law, hero frame and specific lesson for each Peelerz vignette.
