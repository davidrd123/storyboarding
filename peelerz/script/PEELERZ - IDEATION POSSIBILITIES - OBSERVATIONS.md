# PEELERZ - Ideation Possibilities - OBSERVATIONS

Low-inference notes derived from the [source transcription](PEELERZ%20-%20IDEATION%20POSSIBILITIES%20-%20SOURCE.md) and original PDF. These are not source text, creative direction, or a compressed brief.

## Document facts

- The PDF is nine pages and contains 14 embedded color images.
- The title is `PEELERZ - DIRECTORS TREATMENT`; the named author is Ben Hansford.
- Red source text occurs on pages 1, 3, 4, and 6.
- The red text identifies open ideation space, a location revision, and an external video reference.
- The treatment asks for ideas that can be summed up in a few visual styleframes.

## Explicitly open areas in the source

- Page 1 asks what can appear outside Tina's car window and explicitly invites scale changes, including a possible giant hand peeling open the car.
- Page 4 asks what else Raul can transform into and offers multiple-candy Voltron and giant-Raul examples.
- Page 6 states that the worlds Liz and John float through are up to the creative team.

## Actions explicitly stated

- Tina asks whether to peel or eat the candy, then takes a bite.
- The car's top and sides peel away; a parachute opens and lifts Tina.
- Raul finishes peeling a mango Peelerz and puts it in his mouth.
- Raul transforms into ANI-MANGO-RAUL with mango arms and legs.
- Liz and John carry Peelerz bags; the text does not explicitly state that either one peels or eats a candy before their worlds change.
- The three threads converge above the clouds in the final image.

## Structural observations

- The treatment presents three character groupings before the final convergence: Tina; Raul and Meg; Liz and John.
- The proposed changes include an object/environment change, a character transformation, several world or medium changes, and a shared aerial finale.
- Several transitions are named in the source: Tina's lift, the dive into Meg's mouth, emergence over the school quad, a circular dolly, repeated `BOOSH` world changes, and a rise above campus.
- The Liz and John section keeps the couple present while settings or visual media change.

## Unresolved ambiguities and inconsistencies

- `EXT. PARK BENCH` is marked in red as changing to high-school bleachers at a football game, but the treatment's embedded reference image still depicts a park bench.
- Page 8 says `The claymation disappears`, although the immediately preceding written world is described as an underwater paradise and page 7 separately describes flat-shaded old-school animation. The exact claymation beat is not otherwise specified in the extracted text.
- `The candy that Liz & John were riding up` appears in the finale, but the preceding text says only that they rise over campus; the exact candy platform is not separately described.
- The images visualize particular answers to some open prompts, but the red source notes continue to describe those areas as available for ideation.
- The treatment does not specify whether every embedded image is approved direction, provisional visualization, or an example to replace.

## Image/text distinctions

- The page 2 car image includes glossy orange material inside separated car panels; the written sentence specifies only that the car peels away in candy-colored glory and a colorful parachute opens.
- The page 4 transformation image depicts orange anime energy and mango-like eye elements; not all of those details appear in the black treatment text.
- The page 7 grove image contains numerous anthropomorphic figures; the text specifies dancing mangoes and other Peelerz fruit flavors without fixing their exact design.
- Product bags remain visible in many embedded images, but the source text does not state a general rule requiring bag visibility in every transformation.
