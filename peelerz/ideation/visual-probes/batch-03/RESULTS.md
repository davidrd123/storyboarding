## Fast prune

### Advance

- **Moving neighborhood stage-set:** a strong, immediate answer to “what is outside
  Tina's window?” It is weird without being illegible, tactile rather than generically
  magical, and the bored stagehands give it a social/comic register.
- **Campus rotates around the couple:** probably the strongest Liz/John world so far.
  Their relationship stays quiet and anchored while the environment performs the gag.
  It reads in one frame and naturally suggests a camera move.
- **Giant mango echo:** aesthetically stronger and more product-shaped than the plain
  giant Raul, though it loses some of the original “he eats himself” specificity.
- **Different hour at every house:** extremely legible and directly inhabits the view
  outside Tina's window. It is less strange, but useful as an obvious control or a
  transition ladder.

### Keep as material, but do not mistake for a finished idea

- **Social flavor mecha:** the friends now visibly cause the modules, which improves
  the rule. It still looks like candy-colored robot armor; the social authorship is
  more interesting than the final form.
- **Stop-motion pantry campus:** coherent and tactile, but may skew younger/cozier than
  the intended register. It is best treated as a possible medium in the couple's world
  sequence, not yet the sequence's governing idea.

### Stop repairing as single stills

- **Broad peel tether:** the renderer still makes a ribbon attached near the car rather
  than a car surface becoming the ribbon. Another wording pass is unlikely to answer
  the temporal question.
- **Peel redraws the campus arch:** it remains a portal/mask around an illustrated
  background. The actual idea is an action—one known object changing material as a
  boundary crosses it—and likely needs two adjacent frames or a short video test.

## Process finding

“Paintable in one frame” should be a classification, not a universal gate:

- **Premise frames** must read as stills: giant Raul, sideways campus, moving stage-set.
- **Transformation actions** may require a frame pair or short motion proof: car-skin
  becoming ribbon, campus architecture being redrawn.
- **World/medium options** can be judged as stills but do not constitute an idea by
  themselves: stop-motion pantry campus.
