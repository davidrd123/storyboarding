# House-bloom progression — reading and next questions

## The four-frame read

See [progression-strip.png](progression-strip.png).

1. **Ordinary enclosure.** Tina is still absorbed in the gummy. The revised first frame deliberately
   makes the environmental symptom nearly invisible.
2. **Recognition.** Tina turns toward a sequence of houses opening from closed architecture into
   inhabited flowerlike cross-sections. The seam above her window tells us the rule is approaching.
3. **Contagion reaches the car.** The camera moves outside. The car's metal roof and door frame are
   still present, but broad inner petals are pushing through the opened shell.
4. **Inside and outside become one world.** The remaining metal enclosure is gone and Tina sits on a
   mobile flower platform among fully open houses.

The sequence works because the car is treated as another kind of house: a small moving enclosure
around Tina. One transformation rule can therefore travel from the neighborhood to her without a
second arbitrary magic event.

## What the images clarify

The house bloom is strongest when it means **revelation**, not botanical decoration. Closed facades
open to show rooms, gardens, light, and hidden layers of life. Tina's car opening then reveals her in
the same way. The movement is from private interior to shared exterior.

The generated midpoint also suggests a useful construction logic:

- the hard outer shell separates first along clean curved seams;
- a softer petal or membrane structure appears beneath it;
- the structural seat and chassis remain intact;
- the opening is safe and deliberate, never a crash.

The current final image is appealing, but it converts the car almost completely into a flower. A
more Peelerz-specific refinement would keep metallic outer faces on the opened petals and give only
their undersides a translucent fruit-skin or fibrous membrane texture. That would read as a car being
peeled open, not merely a car becoming floral.

## Other Tina dimensions worth testing

### Tina causes the rule

Her hand action can control the outside world. As she pulls a strip from the gummy, a seam travels
across a roof. If she pauses, the opening pauses. If she lays the peel back down, a house almost closes.
This gives her agency and makes the product action legible without explanation.

### The transformation has an advancing edge

Instead of cutting from normal to transformed, a visible boundary can move down the street toward the
car. Behind it, architecture is open; ahead of it, everything is ordinary. This makes the geography
and escalation instantly readable.

### The window is the first threshold

The change might appear only in reflection, then under the glass, then on the far side of it. The
passenger window can behave like a clear peel or membrane before the metal car shell opens. This gives
us a close, tactile transition before the wide exterior reveal.

### The outside world carries hidden stories

Each opened house can reveal something specific rather than another lush garden: a room at a different
time of day, a tiny weather system, an impossible hobby, an interior larger than the building, or
residents calmly continuing their lives inside the bloom. This rewards Tina's bookish observational
quality and keeps the world from becoming generic spectacle.

### Tina and everyone else see different realities

The driver and pedestrians can remain completely matter-of-fact while Tina watches the houses open.
That creates comedy and makes the event feel like her encounter with the product rather than a public
disaster. It also leaves open whether the gummy reveals a hidden world or creates one.

### The car petals become the parachute

The flower platform can be an intermediate state rather than a replacement for the treatment's lift.
After the car opens, its four peeled shell sections could rotate upward, catch air, and join into one
canopy while Tina's seat releases from the chassis. That would make the parachute the final geometry of
the peel, not a new object that appears from nowhere.

## Best next Tina probes

1. **Gesture-controlled transformation:** four frames matching Tina's peel movement to a house and the
   car seam.
2. **Advancing boundary:** one exterior wide shot showing ordinary street, active edge, and opened
   neighborhood simultaneously.
3. **Car peel design study:** metallic fruit skin, architectural panels, translucent window membrane,
   and shell-to-parachute geometry.
4. **Hidden lives in open houses:** several specific interiors based on what Tina might find absorbing,
   funny, or beautiful.

## How this extends to the other encounters

The same method can be used without forcing the same visual language onto everyone:

- **Raul and Meg:** ordinary bleachers, first precise symptom, Raul's power fantasy peaks, then Meg
  actively answers it with her own transition rather than remaining a reaction shot.
- **Liz and John:** ordinary campus, a shared choice or gesture triggers one world rule, the rule
  develops through several media, and their final ascent grows out of the last transformation.
- **Finale:** each thread should arrive carrying a recognizable remnant of its own rule—Tina's peeled
  canopy, Raul and Meg's forms, and the couple's transformed platform—so convergence feels earned.

## Model notes

- The original four-job run returned frames 1, 2, and 4; frame 3 returned no image once.
- Interpolating between the successful frames 2 and 4 produced a convincing midpoint on the first
  retry.
- Using the strong bloom image as a reference made the original opening frame transform too much too
  soon. Removing that reference produced the properly restrained opening used in the strip.
- This reinforces the earlier result: references improve continuity, but each reference also exerts
  narrative pressure. Fewer references can be better when the desired beat is subtle.
