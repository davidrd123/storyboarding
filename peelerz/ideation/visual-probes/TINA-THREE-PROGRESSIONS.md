# Tina — three four-frame progressions

See all twelve selected frames together in
[tina-three-progressions.png](tina-three-progressions.png).

## A — Houses bloom

**Rule:** Enclosures reveal their hidden interiors. Houses open first; the car follows because it is
another enclosure around Tina.

**What it gives us:** The richest world and the most thematic reading. The neighborhood contains
hidden lives, and Tina becomes newly visible as her own enclosure opens. This best supports her as an
observant, bookish audience-identification character.

**Risk:** The car becomes a literal flower too quickly. A refinement should preserve blue-gray metal
on the petal exteriors and use organic fruit membrane only on the undersides, so the action remains a
peel rather than a floral makeover.

## B — Giant hand and miniature street

**Rule:** Tina's ordinary world is a living miniature that can be carefully handled from outside.

**What it gives us:** The clearest physical peeling action, the boldest scale joke, and a direct answer
to Ben's giant-hand prompt. The hand makes the car peel concrete rather than metaphorical. It is also
visually legible without a complicated fantasy environment.

**Risk:** Revealing the whole neighborhood as a miniature is a major cosmological turn. The last two
beats are compositionally similar, and the giant observer may become more interesting than Tina. This
works best if her reaction or choice controls what the hand does next.

## C — Sky peel becomes canopy

**Rule:** The ordinary afternoon sky is a flexible outer skin. Once peeled, it catches the car shell
and inflates into the same material that lifts Tina.

**What it gives us:** The cleanest causal bridge from the world outside to the car peel and then to the
required parachute flight. The canopy does not arrive as an unrelated prop; it is the final geometry of
the peeled sky and car. The pearl exterior and translucent pink interior feel especially appropriate
to lychee.

**Risk:** The fruit-night world beneath the sky is overdecorated in the current frames. The action
would become more mysterious and premium if the revealed atmosphere contained only two or three
specific impossible elements rather than a field of fruit symbols and floating islands.

## Comparative read

- **Most character- and world-rich:** Houses bloom.
- **Most surprising single image:** Giant hand.
- **Strongest four-beat commercial mechanics:** Sky peel.
- **Closest to the written treatment while improving its logic:** Sky peel.
- **Best basis for exploring Tina's personal perspective:** Houses bloom.

The most promising synthesis is not to combine all three spectacles. It is to use the **house
sequence's patient onset and Tina reaction**, then the **sky sequence's material logic for opening the
car and forming the canopy**. The giant hand should remain a distinct comic alternative rather than
enter that combined version.

## Generation notes

All frames were generated with `gemini-3.1-flash-lite-image`, at 1K and 16:9, using explicitly labeled
reference roles. The selected midpoint in each sequence was generated by interpolating between its
successful preceding and following frames when the initial action frame was missing or insufficiently
open.
