# First-pass review

All 12 prompts produced a 1K, 16:9 frame. Job 12 encountered one transient API
error and succeeded unchanged on retry.

## Strong visual ingredients

- **03 — exterior becomes a little tropical model:** useful inside/outside
  composition; the real car frame and miniature street coexist clearly.
- **05 — exterior wide:** clean proof of a full-size car moving through the
  small tropical neighborhood. Simple and immediately readable.
- **06 — miniature world at night:** strongest lighting variant; keeps the
  sparse fish and miniature houses without adding story.
- **07 — giant hand arrives:** useful hand/car/world scale relationship and a
  clear anticipation beat, although the open door should not carry forward.
- **11 — peel unfurls into canopy:** strongest parachute-family image; light
  through the pink membrane is valuable even though material continuity needs
  refinement.

## Useful but close to the existing keeper

- **01 — interior hero:** strong continuity reproduction, little new discovery.
- **02 — change begins in the light:** restrained and plausible, but the change
  is very slight.
- **04 — fish cross the window:** the boundary-crossing idea reads, though it
  needs a cleaner distinction between fish inside and outside.
- **08 — giant hand peels:** evocative hand and pink membrane, but reads partly
  as the hand placing or removing a lychee rather than peeling the car shell.

## Mechanics misses

- **09 — peeled-open hero:** reintroduces orange gummy upholstery and the car
  reads as disassembled rather than cleanly peeled.
- **10 — roof peel catches air:** pink material reads as a blob placed on the
  roof, not the car skin lifting.
- **12 — parachute hero:** becomes a conventional parachute suspension image;
  the continuous car-skin transformation is lost.

The run supports using Nano Banana for reference-guided lighting, production
design, and composition probes. It remains unreliable when a single frame must
explain the exact peel-to-canopy material logic.
