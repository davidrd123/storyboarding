# Miniature music-video progression — results

See [progression-strip.png](progression-strip.png).

## Read

1. **Light arrives first.** Cyan exterior light and pink lychee bounce begin changing the live-action
   car before the production design changes.
2. **The street becomes a little set.** Pastel bungalow models, sparse palms, turquoise cyc, and a few
   suspended gummy fish replace the real exterior without overwhelming Tina.
3. **The car joins the stage language.** Its outer skin curls open as two smooth scenic panels with a
   pearly pink underside. The miniature world remains shallow and frontal behind her.
4. **The construction is revealed.** Tina, the rear seat, opened car panels, miniature neighborhood,
   suspension lines, lighting stands, and black stage edges become one deliberately artificial image.

## What works

- The transformation reaches Tina first through **colored light**, then through objects, then through
  the car itself.
- Live-action Tina against a visibly handmade environment creates more tension and charm than making
  every element share one seamless fantasy realism.
- The suspended gummy fish connect lychee color and material to the tropical set while lightly
  foreshadowing underwater imagery.
- The opened shell reads simultaneously as car peel, lychee skin, and removable scenic flat.
- The final frame's visible stage edges make the music-video construction feel intentional rather than
  like an imperfect illusion.
- The restricted inventory—four houses, a few palms, several fish, one turquoise field—lets the
  lighting and silhouette carry the idea.

## Current judgment

This is one of the strongest Tina directions so far, especially if production simplicity and a
slightly heightened music-video tone are desirable. It does not need a cosmological explanation. Its
rule is simply that Tina's real car is passing into a handmade Peelerz set, and eventually the car
itself becomes part of the set construction.

The next refinement should concentrate on lighting variations and physical car-panel design rather
than adding more world elements.
