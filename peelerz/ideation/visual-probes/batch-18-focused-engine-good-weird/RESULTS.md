# Batch 18 results — first render gate

The first focused-engine pilot produced twelve useful pieces of evidence. It
did not yet produce six presentation images. The central finding is that the
conceptual seeds are more Peelerz-specific than the original engine, but their
metaphor nouns still become powerful render attractors.

## Read by mechanism

### 01 — sugar-frost seam propagation

Best: `01-sugar-frost-lotus-rig_01.png`.

The image earns a reference slot. It gives us the right giant-car / tiny-house
scale, coordinated lychee population, visible tension lines, Tina's engaged
delight, and a lovely pink transformed material. It does not fully prove that
car skin follows the seams: the model literalized “lotus” as a flower mounted
on the roof. Useful error: this is the correct image of helpers conducting the
opening, but not yet the correct topology of car-to-canopy transformation.

### 02 — reflection changes before reality

Best composition: `02-reflection-peels-first_02.png`.

Both variants are handsome, moody frames. Neither obeys the optical rule. The
second car is physically present behind Tina rather than existing only inside
the window/puddle reflection, and the peel ribbon enters the real car without
a readable reflective boundary. Useful error: this is the correct image of a
transformed car following Tina through a rainy night. The feral idea survives
conceptually but needs a more exact compositing brief or manual construction.

### 03 — Meg holds the assembly signal

Best mechanism sketch: `03-meg-holds-the-assembly-signal_01.png`; best Raul
state: `_02.png`.

Meg's bodily participation is finally readable, and the giant opened mango
creates welcome space around Raul. The renderer substitutes grape familiars
along the connector and treats the five panels as flower/wing scenery. The
actual component-to-joint chain disappears. Useful error: this is the correct
image of Meg actively pulling Raul's spectacle into being, which is worth
keeping even though the assembly mechanics need recompilation.

### 04 — each bite replays a component

Best: `04-every-bite-is-a-replayed-component_02.png` for the screen-to-panel
action; `_01.png` for the clean wide geometry.

This is the strongest conceptual success. The scoreboard replay visibly
causes an armor component to enter the stadium world, and the football
formation is clean enough to support a second effect. The gummy is rendered as
ordinary mango/banana fruit, the players are not convincingly weightless, and
the component does not visibly meet an open joint. Still, it gives Ben a new
discussion object: bite-by-bite public assembly through sports replay grammar.

### 05 — helpers as load-bearing gaps

Best evidence: `05-helpers-are-temporary-joints_02.png`.

The model understands a chain of tiny characters adjacent to Raul's joints,
but not that the chain is carrying force across separated components. In one
variant grape characters tether Raul to Meg; in the other mango helpers form a
decorative knee garland. The mango-flesh surface is improved, while the suit
still collapses toward superhero/bodybuilder anatomy. Useful error: the image
is a clean diagnostic of exactly what “decorative helpers” looks like.

### 06 — canopy/scoreboard arc

Best: `06-canopy-arc-becomes-scoreboard-arc_01.png`.

The transition ingredient is visually strong: a tactile pink-to-rainbow arch
with colored lines continuing into the field. It is also the purest attractor
failure. “Arc” becomes a freestanding rainbow gate; the car-to-canopy lineage
is absent, and the mecha loses Raul's head. Useful error: this is the correct
image of a silent color-and-shape bridge, not a finished Raul scene.

## What the pilot changes in the engine

The distance rings did produce different concepts, but usefulness did not
increase with conceptual distance. More importantly, source-domain metaphors
survived too far downstream:

- lotus → literal flower;
- lighthouse signal → glowing-rope pageant;
- temporary joint → decorative character chain;
- canopy/scoreboard arc → literal rainbow gateway.

The engine now has a Gate 2.5 render compiler. It preserves metaphor during
divergence, then removes metaphor and source-domain nouns from the render
prompt. Selected ideas are expressed as substrate, initial state, applied
force, topology, state change, residue, shot proof, and literalization hazards.
