# First-pass review

The pressure test succeeds: these are recognizably different pitches, not
twelve treatments of one car-peel board. The strongest ideas also reveal the
next filter. Openness is productive when the situation remains connected to
Tina, her line, or the physical behavior of Peelerz.

## Strongest situations

- **04 — three Tinas, three choices:** the most direct extension of `Should I
  peel it? Or eat it?` One coherent image makes the choice multiply Tina and
  gives the creative event a reason to belong to this script.
- **07 — neighborhood peels everything:** a much broader comic situation built
  from the product verb. It could support several concise styleframes without
  requiring lore.
- **06 — city inside the gummy:** product-centered, visually immediate, and
  opens nested scale without depending on the car-peel topology.
- **12 — car becomes a flock:** a genuinely different airborne answer. The
  frame proves that the parachute is not the only legible route from car to
  flight, although the exact transformation would need refinement.

## Strong visual pitches with a weaker Peelerz connection

- **03 — giant Tina:** simple, funny scale play; immediately communicable.
- **05 — Tina redraws live action:** good mixed-media creative register and a
  clear action for Tina.
- **08 — infinite library:** character-specific and visually clean, though
  being bookish may become too literal.
- **09 — accordion street:** strong production-design proposition, but its
  connection to this candy currently comes only from the bite trigger.
- **10 — escaping shadows:** elegant and inexpensive-feeling, but the generated
  frame undersells the independent-shadow action.

## Render misses, not necessarily idea misses

- **01 — frozen time:** Tina moved into the front/driver position and the time
  freeze is represented by too few unmistakable frozen events.
- **02 — sideways gravity:** the rotated exterior reads mostly as a tilted
  camera rather than altered gravity.
- **11 — world drops away:** reads as a rectangular car-seat fragment floating
  above the city, not a stationary Tina with the neighborhood sinking.

## Takeaway

The useful possibility space is wider than the earlier tropical-car branch.
But the strongest new possibilities are not arbitrary. They have a concise
Peelerz-specific organizing verb:

- choice multiplies Tina;
- peeling becomes contagious;
- the gummy contains a world;
- the car metamorphoses into flight.

That is a better selection gate than requiring every idea to share one visual
world or one transformation mechanism.
