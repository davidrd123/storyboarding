# First-pass review

All ten adapted prompts completed successfully with one 1K, 16:9 output each.

## Strong translations

- **03 — launch moment:** the clearest single visual proposition. The hovering
  car, glowing seams, and sprinkler launchpad rhyme all read immediately.
- **07 — birds pull the peel:** the agents, shared peel edge, Tina, and upward
  direction coexist cleanly. This is the strongest new peel-agent image.
- **08 — block repaints itself:** an unusually successful spatial progression
  from gray houses to Cuban color, with the car centered inside the advancing
  change.
- **09 — world becomes painting:** clean separation between photoreal car and
  impasto exterior; Tina and product remain strong.
- **10 — driver does not notice:** the deadpan register reads, Tina stays in the
  back seat, and the colored roof strips communicate the situation without
  overwhelming the two characters.

## Promising but spatially incomplete

- **01 — stage separation:** produces a strong airborne image and falling car
  forms, but Tina/car/canopy relationships are confused and Tina may be
  duplicated.
- **02 — looking back down:** preserves the back-seat viewpoint and orderly
  parts, but the panels appear on the horizon rather than clearly far below.
- **06 — peel parasail:** attractive and immediately legible as parasailing;
  the tow line and car-to-canopy material relationship remain too weak.

## Misses

- **04 — car outgrows neighborhood:** the car does not convincingly exceed the
  houses in scale; the image mostly becomes a roof-curl variation.
- **05 — seats transform:** the propagation reads, but the gummy material looks
  viscous and organic rather than appetizing translucent upholstery.

The adaptation worked best when the idea could be expressed as one dominant
spatial relationship. Prompts requiring multiple simultaneous depth planes or
an exact material lifecycle will need a second targeted pass rather than more
general description.
