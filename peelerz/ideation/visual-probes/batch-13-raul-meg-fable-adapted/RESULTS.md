# Results — Fable adaptation

All 11 frames completed. Jobs 07–09 initially returned model-internal errors
and succeeded unchanged on retry.

## Strongest material

- **01 — eat center / wear peel:** the most legible expression of product
  physics. The empty peel becomes Raul's glossy arm while tiny mango characters
  help, all within a lively crowd reaction.
- **03 — fireworks coalesce into limbs:** an immediately readable, exuberant
  spectacle. It simplifies the tiny characters into candy units, but the
  assembly gesture lands.
- **05 — mango pit crew:** the helpers give Raul's transformation personality
  and comic behavior instead of merely decorating generic armor.
- **06 — football game goes anime:** useful evidence that Raul's change can
  propagate into the existing game without replacing the setting.
- **10 — green candy tunnel:** a clean, appetizing transition space with a
  strong destination pull.

## What did not quite land

- **02 and 04** drift toward a generic orange superhero/bodybuilder and lose
  some of the peel-specific brand logic.
- **07** has a good comic foreground/background composition, but the model
  introduced a green imp before the story asks for it.
- **08 and 09** establish Meg's creature but do not communicate much action;
  the requested dive becomes another creature-on-palm portrait.
- **11** reads as a generalized candy finale rather than character persistence.

The Fable round is strongest as a Raul mechanism mine. Meg remains mostly a
reaction or transition device, which is the gap the paired pass addresses.
