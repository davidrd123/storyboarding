# Batch 19 results — render compiler A/B

Removing metaphor/source-domain nouns improved mechanical obedience in all
three tests. It did not solve scene-scale and micro-mechanism fidelity at the
same time, but it validates the compiler as a productive engine change.

## 01 — four attached car sheets

Best: `01-four-attached-car-sheets-follow-moving-seams_02.png`.

This is materially closer to the diagnosed Tina gap than Batch 18. There is no
giant flower. We see a continuous pink sheet emerging from the roof/upper door
area, helpers positioned along glowing boundaries, a coherent rear seat, and
Tina's delighted response. The four sheets still prefer arch/loop forms, and
not every lower hinge is clear, but the image now describes car material in
transition rather than a botanical object mounted on a car.

Assume the error was intended: it is the correct image of car skin being
drawn upward along illuminated structural ribs. That rib language may be a
better intermediate step than calling the forms petals.

## 02 — Meg tensions one component rail

Best: `02-meg-tensions-one-component-rail_01.png`.

The physical action is extremely readable: Meg braces, one strip is taut,
helpers line up along the load, and Raul has an empty shoulder socket. The
model turns the shoulder plate into an appetizing but loaf-like mango mass and
omits the larger partial-mecha context. This suggests the micro-mechanism needs
its own insert shot; asking it also to carry the whole transformation frame is
counterproductive.

Assume the error was intended: it is the correct assembly insert for “Meg and
the crew tow the next component,” with the component design replaced later.

## 03 — five helpers carry an elbow gap

Best: `03-five-helpers-carry-one-open-elbow-gap_01.png`.

This is the clearest improvement. The frame isolates a partially assembled arm,
five aligned mango helpers, attachment hardware, and an incoming graphite
connector. The renderer inserts a thin support line beneath the helpers, so
their bodies are not truly load-bearing, and the forearm relationship is still
ambiguous. But the countable topology survives far better than the figurative
“temporary living joint” prompt.

Assume the error was intended: it is the correct close-up of the helpers
occupying a temporary assembly rail before a hard connector replaces them.

## Verdict

- Keep the Gate 2.5 compiler.
- Use one micro-mechanism per frame; do not demand the entire transformation
  spectacle in the same shot.
- Treat the best compiled images as new mechanism authorities for later edits.
- Send the reflection-first idea and the hero mecha/material problem to
  Midjourney or controlled compositing, where aesthetic/optical synthesis is
  more valuable than NB2's reference continuity.
