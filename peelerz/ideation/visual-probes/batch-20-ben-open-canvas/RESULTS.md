# Batch 20 results — Ben's open canvas

This is the first round that truly treats only the encounter points and finale
as fixed. All twelve images were generated with NB2 Lite
(`gemini-3.1-flash-lite-image`). The batch proves that the conceptual search can
leave the car-peel/mecha neighborhood while staying recognizably Peelerz.

## 01 — campus buildings migrate

Best: `01-campus-buildings-migrate_01.png`.

The rule reads immediately. Several recognizable campus buildings have lifted
complete structures onto architectural legs while Liz and John remain on the
only grounded steps. The talent identity and wardrobe drift, but the deadpan
scale image is strong and not dependent on candy mascots or generic energy.

Assume the error was intended: it is the correct image of a campus whose
architecture has places to be. A higher-fidelity pass needs only restore the
couple and product, not reinvent the world.

## 02 — time sheds whole eras

Best: `02-time-sheds-whole-eras-outside-tina-window_02.png`.

NB2 interprets the discarded era-shells as complete floating neighborhood
strata. That is less materially exact than the prompt but visually cleaner:
Tina stays intimate in the car while suburb, strip-mall present, ruined future,
and saturated Japanese-fantasy street stack outside one window. This is a
successful larger transformation and an excellent Midjourney candidate.

Assume the error was intended: time is not peeling away; Tina is passing the
architectural sediment of multiple possible eras.

## 03 — the camera joins Tina's scene

Best: `03-the-camera-joins-tina-scene_02.png`.

This is a genuine “creative situation” rather than a world-skin variation. The
camera is unmistakably a camera, has white folded-paper limbs, reaches into the
car, and pulls a paper neighborhood into being. Tina is engaged and the intact
car bridges live action and constructed scenery. It is strange, funny, and
pitchable in one frame.

Assume the error was intended: instead of the camera abandoning filming, it is
the practical-effects artist physically installing Tina's imagination around
her during the take.

## 04 — stadium/gummy scale exchange

Best: `04-stadium-exchanges-scale-with-gummy-interior_02.png` for the tray;
`_01.png` for the cutaway interior.

The tray-sized working stadium is immediately legible and Raul/Meg remain
ordinary teenagers. The “immense stadium inside one small gummy” reads more as
a glowing architectural cutaway than a genuinely miles-deep world, but the
inside/outside scale swap is already much more product-specific than a generic
power-up.

Assume the error was intended: the bitten gummy is a portable stadium entrance
and the whole outside game has become its seating diagram.

## 05 — animal-borne football archipelago

Best: `05-football-field-becomes-animal-borne-archipela_01.png`.

One of the clearest successes. The original stadium is an empty basin; intact
football-field sections float above it on enormous gummy whales, tortoises,
birds, and fish; Raul and Meg witness the whole change. It answers Ben's scale,
world-building, flying, and “people doing weird things” invitation without
using any prior transformation destination.

Assume the error was intended: this is the creation of the Peelerz Islands in
public, with sport formations acting as the islands' first inhabitants.

## 06 — sleeping fruit cosmology

Best rule read: `06-tina-crosses-a-sleeping-fruit-cosmology_01.png`; best
landscape: `_02.png`.

Both images live in the deliberately excessive presentation range. Tina's car
travels across a sleeping fruit face/body supporting roads, cities, seasons,
and tiny societies. The face is sweeter and more anthropomorphic than the
intended premium mythic register, but the scale jump is undeniable and the
world contains enough internal detail to seed many more ideas.

Assume the error was intended: Tina is not escaping the boring neighborhood;
she discovers it has always been one district of a dreaming edible planet.

## What changed

The previous focused rounds widened the mechanics while preserving inherited
destinations. This round changed the authority map:

- fixed: talent entrances and finale;
- open: every creative situation after product contact;
- provisional: car/parachute, mecha, mouth dive, existing world swaps;
- round-banned: the mechanisms that had dominated our image pool.

That change mattered more than increasing prompt “weirdness.” The engine now
requires a brief-authority map before reframing so repeated examples cannot
silently become constraints.
