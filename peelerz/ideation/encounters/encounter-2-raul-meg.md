## Decisions so far

### Meg's flavor: green apple (working choice, 2026-07-13)

- Peach **parked in reserve** — the Momotarō/born-from-the-fruit lane stays
  available as a wild-appendix gift even if green apple holds.
- Lemon **dropped** — not found anywhere in the Peelerz range.
- Flavor lineup evidence (web, 2026-07-13): Amos sells Peelerz in
  mango/lychee/pineapple mixes, a mango/peach/banana/pineapple/lychee
  "Exotic Fruit" mix, and a **lychee/mango/peach/green-apple** assorted
  pouch. Green apple is real; note it appears in the 4-flavor assorted
  SKU, not the tropical mixes. Pineapple remains unclaimed by any
  character (crown/princess grammar noted, unused).

### What green apple buys her

- **Palette separation.** Acid/emerald green against Raul's mango
  amber-gold — her beat can never read as a paler echo of his. (Peach
  would have shared the orange-pink-gold band with both mango and
  Tina's lychee.)
- **Story lineage.** Eden and temptation; Snow White's bite → dark
  sleep → awakening; the apple of discord. The most story-loaded fruit
  in Western art, which feeds directly into the Bosch/Dante mouth
  passage from the transcript.
- **Bosch compatibility.** Bosch's imps are frequently greenish; an
  acid-green gummy imp is nearly period-accurate. The transcript's
  hell-to-paradise passage ("Dante's journey in 0.3 seconds") gets its
  creature and its palette from the same decision.
- **Personality pun.** Green apple is tart. The hard-to-impress girl
  picked the sour flavor. Flavor and character note are one gesture.

### Transition seed this unlocks (hold, don't build yet)

The transcript's Dante line exits Meg's mouth "into the garden of
paradise — the campus quad." That is Encounter 3's location. Green
apple → Eden/garden → campus-quad paradise is a ready-made handoff:
her flavor's mythology *is* the transition's destination. Park until
pass 4 (transitions), but don't lose it.

## Material guardrails that bite hardest on this beat

From product-facts and the transcript's tone fence — these matter more
here than anywhere else in the spot, because a creature entering a
mouth and an interior passage are exactly where candy language can go
wrong:

- **Confectionary, never anatomical** (Codex batch-02 finding). The
  mouth passage must read as candy-lit tunnel/threshold, not throat
  tissue. Jewel-translucent, sugar-dusted, toy-glossy.
- **Appetizing always.** The imp is a gummy someone is about to eat,
  joyfully. It dives in; it is not swallowed in horror.
- **Benign, with comic flirtation with horror** (transcript tone
  field). Bosch density and strangeness, played bright. "Less
  horrifying Attack on Titan" is the calibration.
- **Both layers edible; stretch, not tear** — applies if her peel
  action is ever shown.

### The hard-to-impress dynamic (working)

Meg's dryness flips from render instruction to character trait:
Raul's fantasy is external and loud (the whole stadium sees it),
Meg's is private and stranger (only she and the camera go where hers
goes). This has been the through-line of the flavor choice; adopted
as working characterization, revisable on sight of imagery.
