## The deep unifier: the membrane becomes negotiable

Peelerz is a membrane-object: a skin and a core, and a choice about the
boundary. Eating it transfers **membrane-license** to the eater's world.
Every operation in this spot is an inside/outside operation:

- peeling = inside becomes outside (reveal)
- eating = outside becomes inside (mouth-portal, camera through the mouth)
- escape = getting out of an enclosure (Tina's car)
- transformation = a new self surfacing through the old one (Raul's armor)
- connection = two private interiors becoming one shared outside (the couple)
- the finale = everyone's private inner worlds turn out to share one sky
- scale = the miniature grammar: candy is already a mango shrunk to
  thumb-size; scale play re-inflates the miniature (candy islands, giant hand)
- medium shifts = the film's own skin peels (live action → cel → clay)

## The two-factor test (a seed "makes sense" iff both hold)

1. **Product-physics entailment** — the transformation inherits one of the
   candy's own operations: PEEL / REVEAL / LAYER / CHOOSE (peel-vs-whole) /
   STRETCH-STICK / MINIATURE. Without this: generic wish-fulfillment any
   soda could sponsor.
2. **Exteriorized interiority** — the world does what the moment FEELS like
   to the eater: escape, power/being-seen, intimacy, belonging, mischief,
   awe. Without this: product-demo surrealism, weirdness as wallpaper.

Plus the format gate (Ben's red text): stateable as ONE visible rule,
summable in a styleframe.

Register constraint: benign and reversible — the world is softer, more
layered, more editable than adults claim. No consequence-horror; the
Skittles "cost" beat should read as wry, never cruel.

Ben's own examples, tested: giant hand peels the car = PEEL + escape ✓.
Mango voltron = LAYER + power ✓. Worlds shifting around the couple =
REVEAL/LAYER + new-love-renews-the-world ✓. Islands finale = MINIATURE
(re-inflated) + belonging ✓. The theory reproduces his intuitions.

## The age through-line: the audience is mid-molt

Everyone depicted is 16–20 (Tina is 18; bleachers; campus steps). That age
is itself a membrane state: between childhood and adulthood, between
enclosures (home/away, high school/college), between selves. The product's
physics RHYME with the developmental moment — peeling at 18 is quietly
self-referential: you are the thing whose outer layer is coming off, whose
inside is about to be shown. That's why the peel is satisfying to this
audience in a way it isn't to a 40-year-old.

The four vignettes map to developmental tasks, which is why they're the
fixed anchors:

| Beat | Threshold task | Why the setting |
|---|---|---|
| Tina / back of car | autonomy, first solo transit | a container in motion between places |
| Raul / bleachers | public identity, being witnessed | the social arena, edge of the field |
| Liz & John / campus steps | first connection | between buildings, between classes |
| Finale / sky islands | belonging | everyone's private inner worlds share one sky |

This yields additional "makes sense" material:

- **Substrates from teen interiority** (peel/reveal/miniaturize THESE):
  notebook-margin doodles, phone screens, group chats, lockers, class-period
  bells, borrowed hoodies, yearbook photos, headphone bubbles, curfews,
  learner-permit cars, the last five awkward minutes of class.
- **In-between places** as settings: transit, thresholds, edges, waiting.
  The impossible thing prefers liminal ground.
- **Witness structure** as an escalating axis: alone → seen by one → seen
  by all (matches the treatment's me → witnessed → paired → everyone).
- **What does NOT make sense:** kid-coded whimsy (preschool palettes, fuzzy
  mascots), adult-coded settings (offices, commutes), nostalgia plays. The
  register is the present tense of being seventeen.

## Use in the engine

- BRIDGE (loop step 5) now asks: which product operation does this inherit,
  and which felt state does it exteriorize? Name both or flag the seed.
- ACTIVATE cues should pair one operation + one feeling per concern.
- Wildness budget: be as weird as you like in SUBSTRATE (what gets peeled /
  revealed / miniaturized: shadows, afternoons, aspect ratios, strangers)
  — never in OPERATION (stay inside the six) or REGISTER (stay benign).
  The substrate is where divergence lives; the operation is the brand.
