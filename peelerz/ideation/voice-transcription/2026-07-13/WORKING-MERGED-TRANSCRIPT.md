## Context anchor

Ben's Discord brief says the only fixed encounter points are:

- Tina in the back of a car;
- Raul and Meg in high-school football bleachers;
- Liz and John on campus steps;
- the Peelerz Islands finale.

Everything after the characters eat the candy is open ideation space. Ben wants the feeling of creativity, with still images suitable for storyboards. - Tina bites a Peelerz; her car peels open and a parachute lifts her away.

## High-confidence takeaways preserved by the merge

- The target is youthful but not childish; high-school-age talent gives younger viewers an aspirational point and older viewers a nostalgic one.
- The candy's felt promise is not literal “creativity” by the characters so much as magic, transformation, surprise, and an authored imagination-world.
- The three beats already form an escalation: individual escape, individual power fantasy, then shared relational/world transformation.
- Environmental change can start subtly in beats one and two and become total in beat three.
- Tina's transformation can travel from the unnoticed outside world inward until the car itself peels.
- The work needs dream-logic connective tissue, not merely unrelated spectacular styleframes.
- References should function as generative ingredients and Easter eggs, not just recognizable imitation.
- Explore beyond the final usable range. The deliberately excessive or “wrong” versions can reveal a useful transition, texture, composition, or character idea.
- Raul has a full transformation while Meg currently functions mainly as reaction and transition. Giving Meg an active gummy transformation is a major opportunity.
- The mouth transition can become motivated if a transformed gummy character deliberately enters Meg's mouth and carries the camera onward.
- Use the whole mango vocabulary—fruit, peel, tree, branches, juice, candy—not only mango-shaped gummies when exploring Ani-Mango-Raul.
- A wider Gummyverse allows gummy fish, gummy fairies, fruit societies, mythology, and other flavors without insisting that every object be the advertised product.
- Tropical surface motifs can rhyme across worlds: glowing mangoes become glowing gummy fish; trees become kelp; similar framing can make transitions legible.
- The best working process here is capture first and edit later: build a wide visual/story world, then reduce it to the few styleframes Ben needs.
