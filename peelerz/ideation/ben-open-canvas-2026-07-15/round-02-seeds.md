# Open-canvas round 2 — displaced worlds

External entropy was drawn with seeds 2201–2203. Four configurations bind one
concrete noun, one far domain, and one oblique instruction; each is forced
through all three encounter spaces before comparison.

The twenty mechanisms from round 1 are round-banned here.

## Configuration A

**DRAW:** contact sheet / beekeeping / “Discard an axiom.”

The discarded axiom is that only one version of a moment can physically occupy
the world.

### A1 — Tina drives through the hive of possible days

**RULE:** The neighborhood becomes a vast physical grid of full-size alternate
days separated by street-wide gutters; camera-like pollinators carry one detail
from each day into the next until the possibilities crossbreed.

**FRAME:** Tina's intact car is tiny on a black gutter-road passing through a
city-sized grid: rainy childhood suburb, desert future, night carnival, ocean
street, fruit metropolis, ordinary Tuesday. Thousands of small lens-bodied
pollinators move colored architectural fragments between neighboring worlds.

**BRIDGE:** Peelerz choice opens simultaneous possibilities rather than one
correct answer. **LINEAGE:** morphological collision / contact sheet +
beekeeping + oblique / no parent.

### A2 — every possible football play exists at once

**RULE:** The stadium becomes a spatial overview of every possible play, with
full-size fields stacked through the sky; crowd attention pollinates one field
until that possibility grows larger and becomes the current game.

**FRAME:** Raul and Meg sit at the center of a three-dimensional stadium hive.
Dozens of complete football fields occupy luminous rectangular cells above,
below, and around them, each running a different play; streams of colored crowd
attention move like pollination between cells; one chosen field is swelling to
stadium scale.

**BRIDGE:** Choosing how to eat the candy becomes choosing which reality gets
to become full-size. **LINEAGE:** morphological collision / configuration A /
no parent.

### A3 — Liz and John crossbreed whole worlds

**RULE:** Campus is rebuilt from walkable world-thumbnails; whenever Liz and
John look together at two adjacent worlds, flavor-colored pollination carries
one feature across the gutter and creates a third hybrid world.

**FRAME:** The couple stands on a broad black seam between a moonlit paper ocean
and a live-action tropical library; luminous pollen carries paper waves into
the library trees while books become fish in the ocean. Behind them, a physical
grid of other full-scale worlds recedes across campus.

**BRIDGE:** Their shared attention becomes creative combination rather than a
sequence of unrelated portal destinations. **LINEAGE:** morphological collision
/ configuration A / no parent.

## Configuration B

**DRAW:** trellis / customs inspection / “Are there sections? Consider transitions.”

### B1 — the city becomes a vertical road trellis

**RULE:** Los Angeles road sections rise into a kilometer-high lattice; every
intersection is a customs gate that admits a different law of gravity, so
traffic changes orientation without any vehicle transforming.

**FRAME:** Tina's ordinary car drives from a horizontal street onto one vertical
road, while nearby lanes curve upside-down and sideways across an immense city
trellis. Tiny customs booths occupy every joint and inspect colored physical-
law tokens; buildings grow from all faces of the lattice.

**BRIDGE:** A candy with separable layers becomes permission to cross between
different physical rules. **LINEAGE:** morphological collision / trellis +
customs + oblique / no parent.

### B2 — football routes become a climbable sky lattice

**RULE:** Every route in the playbook rises from the turf as a solid walkable
path; referees at each junction inspect which laws of motion the next section
allows, and players continue the game vertically through the sky.

**FRAME:** From Raul and Meg's high seat, the field has grown into a vast
three-dimensional lattice of colored route-lines. Players run up walls, across
inverted sections, and around loops while striped customs-referees examine
momentum at small junction desks. The original flat yard lines remain the root.

**BRIDGE:** Peelerz turns the imagined diagram of a play into inhabitable
creative space. **LINEAGE:** morphological collision / configuration B / no parent.

### B3 — each campus section removes one assumption

**RULE:** Liz and John walk through successive customs frames suspended on a
world-trellis; each checkpoint removes the next apparently unimportant element
of reality—signs, background people, shadows, depth, then gravity—until only the
couple, their gesture, and flavor color remain.

**FRAME:** One long perspective shows six campus sections threaded along a
white lattice. The near section is live action; successive sections visibly
lose typography, extras, shadows, perspective, and finally the ground. Liz and
John span two sections at once, still reaching toward each other.

**BRIDGE:** The candy strips away the world's noise until recognition is the
only surviving rule. **LINEAGE:** morphological collision / configuration B /
no parent.

## Configuration C

**DRAW:** sandbar / swarm robotics / “Repetition is a form of change.”

### C1 — Los Angeles is rebuilt between flavor tides

**RULE:** Tina's neighborhood is a temporary sandbar between two enormous
translucent flavor oceans; each repeated tide releases a coordinated candy
swarm that rebuilds the city in a new arrangement before the next wave.

**FRAME:** Tina's intact car pauses on the only dry road. A pale lychee sea and
gold mango sea tower on either side without crashing. Behind the retreating
tide, millions of small geometric candies interlock into a new street grid,
houses, palms, and overpasses; several previous city arrangements remain as
submerged ruins.

**BRIDGE:** Repeated bites do not repeat the same pleasure; they recompose the
familiar world. **LINEAGE:** morphological collision / sandbar + swarm robotics
+ oblique / no parent.

### C2 — repeated plays deposit new geological fields

**RULE:** Every time the football team repeats a play, the field deposits a new
full-size turf layer above the last one; coordinated candy swarms stabilize the
edges until the stadium becomes a vertical geology of remembered games.

**FRAME:** Raul and Meg look across a canyon of twelve complete football fields
stacked one above another, each with a live play frozen at a different phase.
Tiny dense candy units form structural seams between layers while the current
field is visibly growing from fresh yard-line sediment.

**BRIDGE:** Repetition becomes accumulation and visible memory, not another
anime power effect. **LINEAGE:** morphological collision / configuration C /
no parent.

### C3 — every shared step creates temporary land

**RULE:** Each time Liz and John repeat the other's walking rhythm, a new island
assembles under the next step; diverging rhythms create separate archipelagos,
and synchronized steps cause the islands to join.

**FRAME:** The campus has vanished beneath a shallow luminous sea. Behind each
student trails a different chain of precisely assembled islands made from
campus objects and candy pieces; their next synchronized footfall is joining
both chains into one large inhabited quad.

**BRIDGE:** Connection becomes literal co-authored ground. **LINEAGE:**
morphological collision / configuration C / no parent.

## Configuration D

**DRAW:** pod / competitive falconry / “Take away the elements in order of
apparent non-importance.”

### D1 — Tina's neighborhood is one traveling world-pod

**RULE:** The ordinary neighborhood is the interior of one translucent seed pod
carried across the upper atmosphere by a colossal gentle bird; as “unimportant”
background elements fall away, Tina sees that every remaining detail is part of
the pod's navigation system.

**FRAME:** Cutaway super-wide: an enormous lychee-pink pod hangs beneath a
crane-like gummy bird above Earth. Inside it, Tina's complete street, car,
houses, trees, and people curve along the pod wall. Loose signs and decorative
objects drift away, revealing roads as glowing flight instruments.

**BRIDGE:** The background world was secretly a vehicle generated by flavor.
**LINEAGE:** morphological collision / pod + falconry + oblique / no parent.

### D2 — rival sky birds carry competing stadium pods

**RULE:** The football game is a selection ritual among colossal gummy birds,
each carrying a stadium-sized flavor pod; every play removes one unnecessary
feature from its stadium until the pure winning rule remains.

**FRAME:** Raul and Meg watch five enormous birds circle above the school, each
bearing a different translucent pod containing a complete variation of the
stadium. One pod has lost the crowd, another the sidelines, another gravity;
players continue in every simplified world.

**BRIDGE:** Flavor worlds compete by creative subtraction rather than combat.
**LINEAGE:** morphological collision / configuration D / no parent.

### D3 — world-pods replace campus during a sky exchange

**RULE:** Giant birds exchange sealed world-pods above Liz and John; when a pod
lands, it removes the current campus element by element and replaces it with
the complete world stored inside.

**FRAME:** A bird releases a translucent pod directly above the couple. The
left half of campus is progressively disappearing—cars, extras, signs,
buildings—while the right half emerges as an intricate night ocean-city from
inside the descending pod. Other birds carry unopened genre worlds overhead.

**BRIDGE:** Each candy is a portable authored world capable of taking the
stage. **LINEAGE:** morphological collision / configuration D / no parent.
