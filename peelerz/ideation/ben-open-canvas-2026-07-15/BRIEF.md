## Correction to the previous round

The focused-engine pilot treated the treatment's provisional destinations as
constraints: Tina's car had to become a parachute and Raul had to become a
mango mecha. That made useful execution studies, but it explored inside
existing answers instead of Ben's open space.

This round fixes only the entrances and finale. Everything between them may be
replaced.

## Four frames

1. **World event** — a bite changes the entire external situation, not merely
   the candy, car, or eater.
2. **Authored medium** — reality changes who or what is making it, or changes
   visual/narrative medium as an active event.
3. **Role exchange** — eater, audience, environment, camera, and product may
   trade scale, identity, agency, or narrative position.
4. **Gummyverse myth** — behave as if we are designing a ninety-second hidden
   saga or civilization, then harvest its best few styleframes.

## Round bans

These are not rejected globally; they are prohibited in this divergence batch
because they already dominate the pool:

- car skin peeling into a parachute;
- giant hand opening a car;
- mango-mecha or Voltron assembly;
- helpers acting as a pit crew;
- scoreboard releasing components;
- flowers, petals, lotus openings, or blossoming houses;
- rainbow arches or ordinary rainbow parachutes;
- simple “people float” levitation;
- gummy crowds whose only action is cheering;
- a transformed duplicate car appearing beside the real car.

## Contractual anchors

- Youth-coded, not infantile.
- Benign, appetizing candy; no body horror.
- The product action or product world remains a plausible bridge, but need not
  be a literal causal trigger in every direction.
- A direction can be excessive or impossible to shoot if it gives Ben a great
  image, transition, world rule, or source of joy.
- Every survivor declares whether it is one still, a paired still, or a short
  visual sequence.
