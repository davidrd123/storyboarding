# Gate — open-canvas round 2

## Six render survivors

| Seed | Why render it |
| --- | --- |
| A1 — hive of possible days | Makes multiple possibilities a physical city rather than a graphic collage; strongest Tina-scale expansion. |
| A2 — every football play at once | Turns sport into a spatial multiverse organized by attention, with no mecha or field-island repetition. |
| B3 — customs of subtraction | A transformation by progressive removal; visually and rhythmically unlike the additive spectacle elsewhere. |
| C1 — city between flavor tides | Large sensory/product world with clear reconstruction action and appetizing material. |
| C2 — geological fields | Repetition becomes vertical memory; a single super-wide can sell the rule. |
| D3 — exchanged world-pods | Gives Liz and John multiple authored worlds through one huge sky ritual rather than portals or a camera orbit. |

## Held

- B1 and B2 are strong but share large lattice geometry; B3 is the less
  predictable transformation.
- C3 risks reading as another floating-island route after Batch 20.
- D1 and D2 share giant-carrier imagery with the animal-borne field islands;
  D3 changes the key action to total world replacement.
- A3 is a useful relational platform but less immediately spectacular than A1
  and A2.
