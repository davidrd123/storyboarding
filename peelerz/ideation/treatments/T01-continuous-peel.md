# T01 — The Continuous Peel (from s001)

**Status:** portfolio → treatment. David-starred; Codex-endorsed. The
mechanism, isolated from the image (Codex's three-layer split):
*one peel remains materially continuous as it propagates across
successively larger, unrelated surfaces.*

## RULE
Tina's bite catches the edge of reality, and the car, street, and skyline
peel into one continuous ribbon.

## FRAME (model-neutral; the six fields)
- **Subject:** Tina, 18, calm and lit-up, one hand gripping a broad
  translucent candy-skin ribbon.
- **Action:** the ribbon unspools in one visible, unbroken strip — from the
  passenger seat, through the opened car body, along a lane of asphalt,
  up toward the skyline's lifting edge.
- **Spatial relation:** she hangs above the Uber; the single strip below
  her crosses four scales (seat → car → street → city) without a break.
- **Governing impossibility:** one material strip crosses otherwise
  unrelated surfaces without breaking or changing name.
- **Emotional read:** release WITH tension — escape that stays attached;
  exhilaration, zero fear.
- **Composition:** one strong diagonal ribbon line from lower-right (seat)
  to upper-left (sky-edge); Tina at the upper third; everything off the
  ribbon's path documentary-real dusk.
- **Stranger's caption:** "she's peeling the world like it's the candy."

## LADDER
1. A seam in the passenger seat lifts — the same pink translucency as the
   gummy's skin.
2. The strip pulls through the car's exterior and continues into the
   street without pause.
3. The strip reaches the skyline and begins peeling the city's edge into
   the sky.

## DELTA
One material strip can cross unrelated surfaces without breaking;
everything outside its path remains documentary-real.

## COST (quiet, wry)
The driver keeps driving the peeled chassis, unbothered; his GPS
recalculates forever.

## DIALS
Event 9 / Tina's reaction 3 (calm delight), driver 1. Mismatch 6-8 ✓.

## EXIT (Ben asked for transitions)
The ribbon whips across the lens; when it clears, the same strip IS
Raul's mango peel — or a scoreboard streamer above the bleachers.

## Open production question (Gate 3 probe)
The FRAME's whole selling point is material continuity across scales —
exactly the thing t2i models fail silently at. First render test should
check: does the strip read as ONE object across the four scales, or four
disconnected peels?
