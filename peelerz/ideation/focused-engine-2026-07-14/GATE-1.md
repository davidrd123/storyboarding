# Gate 1 — focused engine pilot

The comparison is deliberately merits-first. Novelty alone is not a reason to
advance: each survivor must make a diagnosed Peelerz gap more visible, physical,
or emotionally useful.

## Six-image pilot

The rendered set is T1, T5, R2, R3, R6, and C2. That gives us:

- two Tina probes: one gap-closing, one feral;
- three Raul probes: adjacent, lateral, and feral;
- one cross-scene visual rhyme;
- three different kinds of "rent": mechanics, image, and story participation.

T2 and T6 remain ready as cleaner/wilder Tina alternates after we see what the
first six reveal.

## Evaluation after images

For each render, ask:

1. Can we state the physical rule from the image alone?
2. Is the strange element caused by Peelerz product behavior rather than generic magic?
3. Does a character actively participate, rather than merely observe?
4. Does the image give Ben a new discussion object?
5. Does it feel like this film, or like a recognizable creativity-engine attractor wearing Peelerz nouns?
