## REFRAME (operator 1)

**Awkwardest truth (what the client would minimize):** the entire product is
packaging. It's candy that imitates fruit and makes you do work before you
may eat it — you are buying the act of unwrapping. The peel is a toy first,
food second. And peeling is SLOW: it installs a delay between wanting and
eating, in a category built on instant gratification.

**Five restatements (problem located in different places):**
- PRODUCT: dramatize the dual-layer physics — the situation IS the peel
  applied at world scale.
- BUYER: teens buy ten watchable seconds of doing something oddly
  satisfying in public — make peeling a flex/spectacle.
- MOMENT: the peel is a delay; make the wait the best part — the situation
  lives inside the delay.
- CATEGORY: candy ads trigger magic on EATING; trigger ours on PEELING —
  reveal is the magic, eating is the button.
- CULTURE: peel-culture (screen protectors, stickers, ASMR) says "fresh
  underneath" — peeling anything reveals its newer, more saturated version.

**Forbidden thing celebrated:** the product's fakeness. Instead of hiding
that it's imitation fruit, celebrate imitation: the world itself turns out
to be a wrapper with something realer/riper underneath.
