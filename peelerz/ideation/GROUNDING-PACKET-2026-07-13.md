# Peelerz grounding packet — four encounters before ideation machinery

**Date:** 2026-07-13  
**Purpose:** Clean starting surface for the restarted creative pass  
**Inputs:** Ben's Discord messages, treatment source/observations, July 13 walk-and-talk

This packet intentionally excludes old seed rankings, campaign laws, creativity-engine categories, and render verdicts. It records what is fixed, what the current treatment proposes, what the human conversation notices, and what remains genuinely open.

## Shared ground

### Ben's assignment

- Infuse the commercial with the **feeling of creativity**.
- After a candy is eaten, a creative situation can be anything.
- Scale, transitions, world-building, flying, and strange behavior are welcome.
- Still images that can function as storyboards are preferred.
- The work is about David's brain, not the particular AI system.
- The fixed elements are where the talent are met: back of car, football bleachers, campus steps, Peelerz Islands finale.

### Audience orientation from the conversation

- Imagine middle-school and high-school viewers, perhaps a high-school freshman as a prototype.
- Characters a little older than the imagined viewer are useful: aspirational, recognizable, and available to nostalgia.
- Youthful does not mean six-year-old or preschool-coded.
- References can be familiar across generations; they do not need to chase the newest meme.

### Meaning of “creative” in this treatment

The transcript's best working translation is:

> Magical, transformative, surprising, a little familiar and a little strange.

The characters do not necessarily need to create art or visibly author the effects. The film's imagination may embody the promise.

### Tone field

- Wordless awe and delight more than dialogue.
- “Whoa” can be enough if the visual event earns it.
- Familiar cultural grammar plus an unfamiliar turn.
- Bold but not generically hyperactive.
- Benign, with room for comic flirtation with horror, myth, or philosophical excess in the exploratory work.
- The workable range and the “not in the final edit, but Ben will enjoy it” range are both legitimate.

### Process

- Capture broadly before editing.
- Establish rough mechanics, then deepen them with references and style.
- Imagine a longer internal saga than the final runtime.
- Use hidden story and character knowledge as visual scaffolding.
- Let visual art and stories both supply imagery.

## Encounter 1 — Tina in the back of the car

### Fixed

- We meet Tina in the back of a car.
- Tina is 18 and described as attractive in a bookish way.
- She has Peelerz.

### Current treatment

- Tina studies a pink lychee Peelerz and asks, “Should I peel it? Or eat it?”
- She bites it.
- The car's top and sides peel away.
- A colorful parachute opens and lifts her into the air.
- Ben explicitly opens the space outside the window and asks about scale and a giant hand peeling the car.

### What the conversation notices

- Tina can function as the audience's identification point.
- The ordinary outside world should be established before it changes.
- The environment might begin gray, boring, or humdrum.
- A transformation could begin outside without Tina noticing.
- Change might travel from the outside toward her until the car itself peels open.
- Versions with and without a giant hand are both worth imagining.
- Beats one and two can show partial environmental transformation before the couple receives total world change.
- If filmed around Los Angeles, choose a boring real landscape that gives transformation somewhere to go.
- Murals could animate or participate.
- Tropical plants, flowers, and fruit could begin appearing in the city.
- The parachute can remain rainbow/flavor-colored, become more directly peel-related, or change through peel types and colors.

### Emotional possibilities

- Release from ordinary enclosure.
- Physical exhilaration.
- Being lifted out of a dull afternoon.
- Discovery arriving before conscious understanding.
- Calm delight amid a large event.

These are possibilities, not a requirement that Tina represent “escape” in every version.

### Visual material already named by the conversation

- Gray city or suburban Los Angeles.
- Vibrant tropical growth encroaching on it.
- Gem-like fruit and saturated flowers.
- Murals coming alive.
- Giant hand / tiny Tina scale contrast.
- A car skin that behaves like fruit peel.
- Rainbow or multi-flavor canopy.
- Outside-in progression.
- *Attack on Titan* scale without its horror.
- A familiar street becoming strange one element at a time.

### Character/story questions

- Where is Tina going before the event?
- Is she bored, observant, distracted, or already imaginative?
- Does she notice the first change or miss it?
- Is she the initiator, recipient, dreamer, or eventual author of the larger chain?
- What does “bookish” contribute besides wardrobe or casting shorthand?
- Does her choice to bite rather than peel matter, or is the line simply the metaphorical opening?

### Open visual questions

- What is outside the window before and after the bite?
- What is the first changed object?
- What makes the movement from street to car legible?
- What does the car become, if anything, before the parachute?
- Is the giant hand comic, wondrous, protective, or merely enormous?
- Does the release happen abruptly or build?
- What single frame—or frame pair—best communicates the contrast?

## Encounter 2 — Raul and Meg in the football bleachers

### Fixed

- We meet Raul and Meg in high-school football bleachers.
- Both have Peelerz.

### Current treatment

- Raul finishes peeling a mango Peelerz and eats it.
- Anime fireworks and lightning erupt.
- Mango arms and legs connect to him as Ani-Mango-Raul, a mango-Voltron figure.
- The world around Raul goes full anime.
- Meg watches in awe.
- The camera dives into Meg's mouth to transition onward.
- Ben explicitly asks what else Raul might transform into and suggests a better candy Voltron or giant Raul eating little Raul.

### What the conversation notices about Raul

- Raul receives a clear individual power fantasy.
- He is carried into the finale in his transformed form.
- The current Voltron/anime shorthand is familiar and usable, but can be varied.
- Giant Raul eating little Raul is funny if handled as “less horrifying Attack on Titan.”
- The transformation vocabulary need not be limited to finished gummies; mango tree, branches, fruit, peel, and the wider mango world are available.
- Other flavors can contribute; mango need not receive every advantage by default.
- Partial environmental change around the bleachers can plant the tropical world that later becomes total.

### What the conversation notices about Meg

- Meg currently receives no comparable imaginative event.
- She exists mainly to marvel and serve as a mouth-shaped transition.
- The mouth dive needs motivation: something should enter her mouth, something should happen to her, or the camera should be following an active event.
- A different flavor can give her a different visual grammar.
- A tiny gummy princess, shōjo counterpart, gummy fairy, or gummy demon are deliberately loose starting points.
- The transformed gummy might joyfully dive into her mouth and carry the camera.
- A Boschian gummy creature could turn the passage into a microscopic hell-to-paradise journey.
- Raul's boy-coded power fantasy and Meg's possible shōjo language can complement rather than duplicate each other.

### Emotional possibilities

For Raul:

- playfully becoming larger than life;
- exuberant self-display;
- being witnessed;
- genre fantasy made physical.

For Meg:

- discovering a private creature or world;
- receiving an invitation rather than merely reacting;
- a complementary transformation;
- choosing to consume or follow something strange;
- comic insistence that she has a soul beyond the transition.

### Visual material already named by the conversation

- Anime power-up.
- Mango Voltron.
- Giant Raul and small Raul.
- Giant gummy or cartoon Raul.
- Multiple flavors.
- Mango-tree or fruit-derived body construction.
- Shōjo contrast.
- Gummy fairy/princess/demon.
- Boschian devouring and transformation.
- A mouth as a passage rather than black filler.
- Football bleachers beginning to sprout tropical or fruit-world elements.

### Character/story questions

- What is Raul like before the transformation?
- What does Meg find delightful, absurd, or personally inviting about what happens?
- Are they friends, a potential couple, siblings, or simply seatmates in the current imagination?
- What flavor does Meg have?
- Does Raul's event cause Meg's, or do two different candy logics coexist?
- What makes the transition into Meg's mouth belong to her rather than use her?
- What fragment of each person's fantasy survives into the finale?

### Open visual questions

- What is surprising beyond “candy-colored robot armor”?
- What is the most fun giant-Raul composition?
- Does Raul transform, or does the stadium transform around an unchanged Raul?
- What does Meg actually hold, peel, eat, meet, or release?
- Can Raul's spectacular hero frame and Meg's active transition coexist without crowding the beat?
- What is the emotional rhythm: Raul peaks, Meg answers, then the camera follows her event?

## Encounter 3 — Liz and John on campus

### Fixed

- We meet Liz and John on campus steps.
- Each carries a Peelerz bag.
- They see one another and connect.

### Current treatment

- Eyes lock and smiles are shared.
- A circular dolly begins around them as they move closer.
- The world becomes a lush mango grove with dancing fruit characters.
- The couple and world shift to flat-shaded old-school animation.
- They enter an underwater paradise with scuba gear and mango-orange fish.
- Claymation is mentioned inconsistently.
- They rise above the campus toward the cloud/island finale.
- The Doctor Strange multiverse fall is the supplied formal reference.

### What the conversation notices

- This relationship is the emotional pinnacle of the current structure.
- Unlike Tina and Raul, Liz and John do not need to transform visibly; the whole shared world transforms.
- Their encounter can represent two people entering a shared imaginative wavelength.
- The current worlds are starting nouns, not finished answers.
- The circular movement can support repeated transitions.
- Doctor Strange uses hard, complete world swaps, but gentler shading, brightening, dimming, or fluxing between styles may feel more attractive here.
- The superhero-film register can feel harsh.
- The couple sequence is permission to move through different media, not only different locations.
- A longer internal version can make each world feel discovered rather than merely checked off.

### Emotional possibilities

- Recognition.
- Shared wavelength.
- The ordinary world feeling renewed by connection.
- Two people staying themselves while reality becomes more permissive.
- Flying or moving together rather than receiving separate fantasies.
- A private connection becoming a whole-world event.

### Visual material already named by the conversation

- Verdant mango forest.
- Gem-like glowing fruit.
- Underwater blue with glowing gummy fish and kelp.
- Tree-to-kelp and fruit-to-fish visual rhymes.
- Flat-shaded animation.
- Claymation or stop motion.
- Studio Ghibli atmosphere.
- *Castle in the Sky* overgrowth and floating-world feeling.
- *House* and 1970s Japanese psychedelic pop-surrealism.
- Bosch and classical still-life Easter eggs.
- A less generic, more historically or artistically specific cartoon language.
- Cutesy 2D gummy characters rather than pseudo-realistic Western 3D fruit faces.
- Brightness/dimming transitions and gradual style flux.
- Characters held in stable composition while materials change.

### Character/story questions

- Why do Liz and John recognize one another?
- Are their imaginative worlds compatible, contrasting, or mutually authored?
- Does each person contribute a different flavor or medium?
- What incrementally changes as they move closer?
- What do they do beyond smile and float?
- What private detail makes the connection feel like theirs rather than generic romance?
- Does anything from Tina or Raul's worlds echo here before the finale?

### Open visual questions

- What are the three or four most specific worlds—not merely colorful categories?
- What remains invariant: body position, eye line, hand movement, camera orbit, color, or composition?
- Do worlds pop, peel, dissolve, dim, redraw, or slowly contaminate one another?
- How can a jungle and underwater world share a visual skeleton?
- What animation or art reference produces an image Ben has not already seen in generic AI work?
- When do they begin flying, and what makes that movement feel like a consequence of connection?

## Encounter 4 — Peelerz Islands finale

### Fixed/current destination

- The threads converge above the clouds.
- The current treatment shows floating islands shaped from Peelerz candies.
- Tina and her parachute, Raul in his transformed form, and Liz and John arrive together.
- Logo and end cards follow.

### What the conversation notices

- The finale confirms that the separate sequences belong to a larger Gummyverse.
- The world need not contain only exact Peelerz products; gummy fish and wider gummy life are compatible with the current treatment.
- Floating-island anime and *Castle in the Sky* are useful reference fields.
- The longer imagined saga can contain lore, societies, monsters, games, or histories that the final super-wide only hints at.
- A generic candyland is less interesting than a place with visual specificity and implied life.

### Emotional possibilities

- Arrival.
- Belonging.
- Recognition that the separate fantasies share a sky.
- A final increase in scale.
- The pleasure of seeing earlier forms return together.
- A glimpse of a much larger world beyond the commercial.

### Visual material already named by the conversation

- Floating islands and airships.
- Overgrown ruins.
- Giant candy/fruit landforms.
- Gummy fish, fairies, monsters, or other inhabitants.
- Tiny mango-gummy societies.
- A pyramid or ritual scene.
- A 16-bit or 32-bit Gummyverse game interpretation.
- Tropical vegetation and jewel-like fruit light.
- Parachute, mango avatar, and flying couple as scale markers.

### Story/world questions

- Is this a destination the characters were moving toward, or a reveal that appears only at the end?
- Is the Gummyverse ancient, newly created, playful, ceremonial, or simply spectacular?
- What do its inhabitants do?
- What makes the world feel larger than one frame?
- How much lore should be visible, and how much should remain private scaffolding?
- Does Tina, Raul/Meg, or the couple contribute anything to opening or completing it?
- Is Meg visibly present in the convergence, and in what transformed or active state?

### Open visual questions

- What distinguishes Peelerz Islands from familiar candyland imagery?
- Are the islands inhabited, functional, or mysterious?
- Is the visual register photoreal composite, animation, painting, miniature, or a final synthesis?
- What earlier visual rhymes return?
- What is the simplest super-wide that implies the richest offscreen world?

## Cross-encounter questions to hold without solving yet

- Is Tina secretly the dreamer or author, or are the encounters independent?
- How does one scene hand its energy to the next without requiring one literal material strip?
- What progression in color, vegetation, scale, and medium makes the spot feel cumulative?
- Does each flavor carry a different mood, creature, or aesthetic language?
- What does Meg bring into the finale?
- What repeated visual rhyme could connect street plants, stadium growth, mango forest, underwater kelp, and floating islands?
- How much of the current script is being enriched, and how much is genuinely replaceable under Ben's “only the meeting places are fixed” statement?
- Which ideas belong in the usable deck, and which belong in a wild appendix intended to unlock and delight?

## First-pass output shape

For each encounter, the restarted ideation should eventually produce:

- **Script-close possibilities:** richer or more specific versions of the current treatment.
- **Lateral possibilities:** changed transformation or hidden story, same human encounter.
- **Wild gifts:** images whose main value is imaginative pleasure or stylistic provocation.
- **Reference field:** a small set of specific visual/story sources and what each contributes.
- **A longer invisible version:** enough imagined sequence to understand what the compressed boards imply.

Selection and product-law questions come afterward.
